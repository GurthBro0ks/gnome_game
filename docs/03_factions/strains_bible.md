# Gnome Game — 5 Strains Bible

## Scope
This document is the next canonical content sheet after the master glossary and the Phase 1 Content Bible.

Its job is to define the **5 core Strains** as playable identity pillars for:
- story tone
- visual direction
- Confidants
- Burrowfolk
- enemy design
- region culture
- event writing
- Deepening reveals

This is not a final stat table. It is a **faction-and-content production guide**.

---

## Core Design Rule
A Strain is never just biology.

In this setting, each Strain is interpreted differently depending on who is talking. A Strain may be treated as:
- an inherited bloodline
- a visible mutation pattern
- a political caste
- a survival strategy
- a spiritual mark
- a propaganda category used by rivals

That ambiguity is part of the setting.

Each Strain should therefore always have:
1. a **public identity**
2. a **private contradiction**
3. a **social role in the present-day world**
4. a **connection to the fall of civilization**
5. a **specific Deepening truth**

---

## Shared World Rule
Old gnome civilization collapsed because the ruling classes betrayed the rest of society, accelerated dangerous invention, and pushed the old world into machine catastrophe and war.

The Strains are part of what came after.

Some people say the Strains were always there.
Some say they were sharpened by fallout.
Some say they were manufactured by old policy and selective breeding.
Some say Deepening proves all of those are true at once.

# 1. Freeroot

## Short identity
Easygoing wanderers, brewers, foragers, coexistence believers, conflict-avoiders, soft-power survivors.

## Public myth
Freeroot are the calm soul of gnomekind.
They travel lightly, share generously, and survive because they do not harden unnecessarily.
They tell the story that civilization survives when people stay flexible.

## Hidden contradiction
Freeroot peace is real, but it often relies on somebody else taking the hit first.
They are masters of sidestepping direct power struggles, which makes them look wise to some and cowardly to others.
They can become experts at moral evasion while sounding harmless.

## Survival philosophy
**Bend, reroute, outlast.**

## Visual identity
- soft travel clothes
- patched satchels
- mushroom dyes
- rope wraps and trail charms
- warm drink flasks
- practical hats that look accidental but are carefully tuned
- scavenged comfort built out of old military leftovers

## Color / material direction
- moss green
- dew blue
- fungus amber
- weathered leather
- damp wood
- soft lantern glow

## Heritage Stat lean
- primary: **Craft**, **Ingenuity**
- secondary: **Renown**
- weak identity: **Order**

## Battle identity
- sustain
- dodge and endure
- indirect wins
- high value from exploration tools and survival loops

## Burrowfolk affinity
- **Hands**
- **Scouts**

## Confidant archetypes
- Pathfinder
- brewer-guide
- salvage wanderer
- cheerful drifter who knows far too much

## Common Wanderer overlap
- Peddlers
- Pilgrims
- Drifters
- Oddfolk

## Region tie
**Loamwake** is their strongest public homeland, but Freeroot appear almost everywhere because they travel, adapt, and barter.

## Main prejudice against them
- unserious
- flaky
- soft
- politically useless
- too willing to make peace with bad actors

## What they say about themselves
“We are the only ones who remember how to live.”

## What rivals say about them
“They survive by staying unblamed.”

## Deepening truth
Freeroot communities were used as buffers and fallback corridors during the collapse. Their flexibility was not just cultural virtue; it was a survival response to being treated as expendable.

## Production notes
Write Freeroot characters as warm, funny, and often observant. Their danger should be subtle: they can normalize bad realities because they are so good at adapting to them.

# 2. Inkroot

## Short identity
Researchers, archivists, investigators, surveyors, record keepers, fact-first obsessives, system explainers.

## Public myth
Inkroot preserve truth.
They know what happened, what matters, and how to keep civilization from collapsing into rumor and chaos.
They are the strain most likely to claim objectivity.

## Hidden contradiction
Inkroot do not merely preserve truth. They sort it, gate it, rank it, redact it, and weaponize it.
An Inkroot institution may honestly believe it is protecting society while deciding which version of reality everyone is allowed to access.

## Survival philosophy
**Catalog first, decide later.**

## Visual identity
- ledger satchels
- measuring tools
- dust masks
- annotation straps
- seal tags
- brass clips and indexing hardware
- field clothes with academic habits

## Color / material direction
- ink black
- brass brown
- parchment cream
- archive red
- survey silver

## Heritage Stat lean
- primary: **Lore**, **Ingenuity**
- secondary: **Order**
- weak identity: **Craft**

## Battle identity
- calculated setups
- information advantage
- precision boosts
- targeting, analysis, and clue-gated strength

## Burrowfolk affinity
- **Keepers**
- **Fixers**

## Confidant archetypes
- Archivist
- survey captain
- records dissident
- obsessive researcher with a conscience problem

## Common Wanderer overlap
- Messengers
- Scrappers
- Peddlers with research motives

## Region tie
**Ledgerhollow** is their symbolic heartland, but Inkroot outposts tend to appear anywhere maps, records, old systems, or classified ruins matter.

## Main prejudice against them
- smug
- controlling
- emotionally dishonest
- addicted to official truth
- incapable of admitting uncertainty without turning it into hierarchy

## What they say about themselves
“If it is not recorded, it can be lost.”

## What rivals say about them
“If it is recorded by them, it can be buried.”

## Deepening truth
Inkroot institutions kept direct records of elite betrayal and wartime escalation. Many of those records were intentionally partitioned, altered, or sealed under “stability doctrine.”

## Production notes
Inkroot should never read as generic nerds. They are truth bureaucrats, evidence aristocrats, and map-minded survivalists. Their best characters are brilliant and dangerous at the same time.

# 3. Brassroot

## Short identity
Builders, labor organizers, industrial pragmatists, machine-tinkerers, “doers,” blunt arguers, infrastructure believers.

## Public myth
Brassroot keep the world running.
They repair, haul, build, reinforce, and push through problems while everyone else debates theory, purity, or memory.
They believe systems should work, and if they do not work, they should be rebuilt harder.

## Hidden contradiction
Brassroot practicality can become brute ideology.
They are vulnerable to certainty, tribal information habits, and “results-first” politics that excuse cruelty as necessity.
When stressed, they may become the best enforcers of bad orders because they cannot stand drift or ambiguity.

## Survival philosophy
**Build through the damage.**

## Visual identity
- reinforced gloves and aprons
- weld marks and soot stains
- obvious tools
- patched industrial coats
- heavy transport belts
- exposed braces, rivets, and utility hats

## Color / material direction
- iron gray
- brass gold
- rust red
- quarry tan
- signal orange accents

## Heritage Stat lean
- primary: **Craft**, **Order**
- secondary: **Ingenuity**
- weak identity: **Lore**

## Battle identity
- straightforward force
- durability
- direct damage
- equipment synergy
- strong region-work and construction bonuses

## Burrowfolk affinity
- **Bruisers**
- **Fixers**
- **Hands**

## Confidant archetypes
- Fixer
- convoy foreman
- stubborn engineer
- practical union-style organizer who keeps building despite everything

## Common Wanderer overlap
- Scrappers
- Messengers
- Peddlers carrying machine salvage

## Region tie
**Wayworks** carries the strongest Brassroot signature, though Brassroot influence also bleeds into any place built on transport, salvage, rebuilding, and machine legacy.

## Main prejudice against them
- loud
- reactionary
- simplistic
- too easy to manipulate with certainty and anger
- convinced labor equals moral clarity

## What they say about themselves
“Somebody has to keep the wheels turning.”

## What rivals say about them
“They will build the cage if you tell them it is necessary.”

## Deepening truth
Brassroot labor and machine guilds were pushed into catastrophic war production under elite pressure, propaganda, and manipulated emergency doctrine. They built much of the system that later broke the world.

## Production notes
Brassroot should feel useful, forceful, and physically grounded. Avoid making them comic brutes. Their best version is proud competence under political misdirection.

# 4. Crownroot

## Short identity
Pureline elites, governors, social stewards, image managers, prestige traditionalists, soft-handed rulers who believe they are necessary.

## Public myth
Crownroot preserve civilization.
They maintain etiquette, continuity, order, and the symbolic structures that keep society from devolving into appetite and panic.
They present themselves as the strain of responsibility.

## Hidden contradiction
Crownroot are deeply entangled with the betrayal that broke the old world.
Their refinement often masks extraction, censorship, favoritism, and narrative control.
They are experts at appearing indispensable while ensuring that dependence continues.

## Survival philosophy
**Rule the story, rule the future.**

## Visual identity
- layered formal clothes
- ceremonial pins and family marks
- polished old court fabrics patched with practical repairs
- heirloom ornaments worn as authority signals
- composed postures and controlled gestures

## Color / material direction
- cream white
- muted gold
- deep wine red
- lacquer black
- pearl gray

## Heritage Stat lean
- primary: **Renown**, **Order**
- secondary: **Lore**
- weak identity: **Craft**

## Battle identity
- command bonuses
- standing-based power
- layered passive effects
- social dominance translated into field pressure

## Burrowfolk affinity
- **Stewards**
- **Bruisers**
- **Keepers**

## Confidant archetypes
- High Confidant
- Court Steward
- family exile with too much insider knowledge
- beautifully mannered political weapon

## Common Wanderer overlap
- Messengers
- Pilgrims of status sites
- Oddfolk nobles in disguise

## Region tie
Crownroot ideology bleeds through all five Strata, but its purest visible expression usually surfaces in ceremonial spaces, prestige systems, and the buried authority structures that Deepening uncovers.

## Main prejudice against them
- manipulative
- entitled
- ornamental parasites
- too polished to trust
- responsible for the world’s breakage

## What they say about themselves
“Someone must keep the standard.”

## What rivals say about them
“They call theft stewardship and expect applause.”

## Deepening truth
Crownroot power blocs were central to the original betrayal. They did not merely fail to stop catastrophe; many actively redirected it to preserve their rank.

## Production notes
Crownroot should feel dangerous even when they are polite. Their dialogue should often sound reasonable at first and rotten a moment later.

# 5. Hushline

## Short identity
Memory keepers, secretive witnesses, concealed prophets, prestige-aware remnants, keepers of details others forget or suppress.

## Public myth
Hushline are half rumor.
Some say they are a strain. Some say they are a role. Some say they are the only gnomes who truly remember each Deepening and each buried layer of the world.
They are treated as eerie, sacred, suspect, or fake depending on who is speaking.

## Hidden contradiction
Hushline do remember more — but memory is not neutral. They may preserve truth, distort it, ration it, or become addicted to the power of being the one who knows what others do not.
Their secrecy protects reality and poisons it at the same time.

## Survival philosophy
**Keep what must not be lost, even if it must be hidden.**

## Visual identity
- muted ceremonial layers
- memory charms
- reed-wrapped relics
- smoke-marked fabrics
- half-practical, half-ritual clothing
- designs that look old even when newly made

## Color / material direction
- fog gray
- candle amber
- old bone white
- marsh green
- soot black

## Heritage Stat lean
- primary: **Lore**, **Renown**
- secondary: **Order**
- weak identity: **Craft**

## Battle identity
- hidden triggers
- echo effects
- delayed power swings
- memory-linked utility and ritual pressure

## Burrowfolk affinity
- **Chantfolk**
- **Keepers**
- **Scouts**

## Confidant archetypes
- Witness
- hush archivist
- veiled guide
- almost-ally who may remember prior Deepenings

## Common Wanderer overlap
- Pilgrims
- Oddfolk
- Messengers who speak in fragments

## Region tie
**Memory Fen** is their most visible cultural anchor, but Hushline are designed to spill beyond a single region. Their major role is to bind the game’s mystery, ritual, and Deepening logic together.

## Main prejudice against them
- manipulative mystics
- liars dressed as prophets
- hoarders of meaning
- impossible to verify
- too close to forbidden history

## What they say about themselves
“Some truths must arrive slowly.”

## What rivals say about them
“They hide behind mystery because mystery cannot be audited.”

## Deepening truth
Hushline are the clearest bridge between strata memory and player memory. They are the strain most likely to make the player suspect that Deepening is not only progression, but recurrence.

## Production notes
Hushline should never become generic spooky seers. Their power comes from selective disclosure, political memory, and ritualized information control.

# Inter-Strain Tension Matrix

## Freeroot vs Inkroot
- Freeroot think Inkroot mistake records for truth.
- Inkroot think Freeroot mistake comfort for wisdom.

## Freeroot vs Brassroot
- Freeroot think Brassroot force solutions too early.
- Brassroot think Freeroot let problems spread by drifting around them.

## Freeroot vs Crownroot
- Freeroot resent Crownroot control.
- Crownroot dismiss Freeroot as unserious social debris.

## Inkroot vs Brassroot
- Inkroot think Brassroot are manipulable.
- Brassroot think Inkroot hide behind paper while others do real work.

## Inkroot vs Crownroot
- Inkroot often serve Crownroot institutions while quietly resenting them.
- Crownroot need Inkroot systems but fear unsanctioned record-keepers.

## Brassroot vs Crownroot
- Brassroot suspect elite contempt.
- Crownroot fear Brassroot anger when it becomes organized.

## Hushline vs everyone
- Everyone projects onto Hushline.
- Hushline see too much and say too little.

# Gameplay Use Rules

## In Strata design
Every Stratum should feel like one Strain won the public argument there, even if another Strain controls the hidden truth.

## In Confidant writing
A Confidant should express the **best case** and **worst case** of their Strain at once.

## In Burrowfolk design
Burrowfolk should reflect what a Strain values when stripped down to work, survival, and obedience.

## In event writing
Each weekly event should reveal how different Strains treat scarcity:
- Freeroot: flexibility and barter
- Inkroot: targeting and information control
- Brassroot: throughput and extraction
- Crownroot: prestige and ranking
- Hushline: memory and hidden cost

## In Deepening
Each Deepening should not merely reveal “more lore.”
It should expose that the Strains themselves are partly products of old policy, old trauma, and old lies.

---

# Production Shortcuts

If a new NPC, enemy, Wanderer, or Confidant is being designed, answer these first:
1. Which Strain do they publicly embody?
2. Which Strain value do they secretly betray?
3. What everyday behavior reveals that contradiction?
4. What old-world lie are they helping continue?
5. What resource, event, or gameplay loop are they naturally tied to?

If those are answered, the character will usually fit the world.

---

# Final Note
The 5 Strains should not read like fantasy races.
They are **survival ideologies wearing bloodline clothing**.
That is what makes them politically sharp, mechanically useful, and expandable across future Deepenings.
