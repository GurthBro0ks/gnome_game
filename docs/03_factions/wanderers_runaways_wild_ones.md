# Gnome Game — Wanderers, Runaways, and The Wild Ones

## Purpose
This document defines the three major roaming NPC/content families that move between the Strata and keep the world socially alive:
- **Wanderers**
- **Runaways**
- **The Wild Ones**

It is a production guide for:
- reusable roaming encounter design
- Burrow Posts / Stranger Posts / Wild Posts content generation
- worldbuilding and faction tone
- travel-route tension in the Strata
- political, social, and strange-content escalation across Deepening

This file assumes the current canon from:
- `master_glossary.md`
- `phase1_content_bible.md`
- `strains_bible.md`
- `clique_confidants_bible.md`
- `deepening_arc.md`
- `duties_and_encounters.md`

---

## Core rule
These three groups should never feel like random flavor NPCs.

They each serve a different world function:
- **Wanderers** make the world feel connected
- **Runaways** make the world feel unstable
- **The Wild Ones** make the world feel dangerous

Together, they explain how a half-broken civilization still moves information, people, goods, rumor, fear, and ambition across fractured Strata.

---

## The three roaming families at a glance

| Family | Public role | Hidden role | Main tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Wanderers** | visitors, peddlers, pilgrims, drifters, messengers | carriers of rumor, pressure, migration, and truth | curious, useful, slightly eerie |
| **Runaways** | defectors, breakaways, dissidents, loose quills, turncoats | living evidence that systems are failing or lying | unstable, sympathetic, politically dangerous |
| **The Wild Ones** | trackers, rakers, chasers, fangs, rousers | enforcers of fear, appetite, frontier violence, or off-book pressure | threatening, theatrical, darkly funny |

---

## Design goals
All three families should support these goals:

1. Make travel between Strata feel active, not abstract
2. Carry news and contradiction without forcing exposition dumps
3. Produce reusable encounter templates for Duties and Posts
4. Let players infer political truth through people in motion
5. Create story pressure before and after Deepening turns
6. Keep the setting socially weird, not just mechanically busy

---

## Wanderers

### Identity
**Wanderers** are the broadest roaming class.
They are the people who still move through a broken world and make that movement look normal.

Some are practical.
Some are harmless.
Some are carrying more than they admit.
Some are valuable because they know how to cross boundaries others cannot.

Wanderers should feel like the living bloodstream of the world.

### Core fantasy
Wanderers embody:
- trade
- rumor
- ritual travel
- displacement
- social exchange
- low-level diplomacy
- strange ordinary survival

### Public function
In UI and authored content, Wanderers can deliver:
- resource offers
- item trades
- route information
- favors
- side Duties
- social color
- low-stakes mystery hooks

### Hidden function
Under the surface, Wanderers also:
- distribute rumor across Cliques
- expose differences between Strata narratives
- reveal migration pressure
- carry forbidden records or objects
- test whether the player is useful, gullible, or dangerous

### Wanderer subtype library

#### Peddlers
Sell useful junk, odd goods, region-bound trinkets, and suspiciously specific necessities.

Use them for:
- soft economy injections
- Treasure Finder hooks
- bundle-like live content without raw shop language

#### Pilgrims
Travel for duty, ritual, memory, grief, or impossible personal reasons.

Use them for:
- Echo Week content
- Memory Fen content
- Smoke Whispers foreshadowing
- Hushline and Witness overlap

#### Drifters
Refugee-coded Wanderers who are surviving one stop at a time.

Use them for:
- sympathy beats
- burdened inventory trades
- Strata instability storytelling
- hard choices around aid and exploitation

#### Messengers
Official or semi-official carriers of sealed information, orders, and deniable instruction.

Use them for:
- Buried Clues
- Clique Queue escalations
- Confidant Posts
- Crownroot intrigue

#### Scrappers
Salvage-minded movement specialists who turn collapse into inventory.

Use them for:
- Salvage Shed loops
- Crack Market foreshadowing
- Wayworks content
- Brassroot overlap

#### Oddfolk
Wanderers whose presentation, story, or behavior is too specific to neatly classify.

Use them for:
- world flavor with hidden teeth
- recurring side characters
- Deepening foreshadowing
- comedy with unease underneath

### Writing rules for Wanderers
Wanderers should usually sound:
- practical
- incomplete
- socially readable
- slightly self-protective
- more informed than they first appear

Avoid making them sound like generic RPG merchants.
They should feel like people crossing active fault lines.

### Wanderer reward profile
Common rewards:
- Mooncaps
- Mushcaps
- Lucky Draws (rarely)
- Treasure Tickets (occasionally)
- Field Cards
- minor Trust
- route clues
- temporary access offers

### Best Strata overlap
- **Loamwake**: first-contact tutorialized social variety
- **Ledgerhollow**: rumor, records, sealed notes
- **Memory Fen**: pilgrims, oddfolk, ritual travel
- **Wayworks**: pure route traffic, escorts, movement logic
- **The Buried Court**: curated, dangerous, politically loaded arrivals

## Runaways

### Identity
**Runaways** are the living proof that not everyone accepts the world as it is.

Some are fleeing law.
Some are fleeing debt.
Some are fleeing truth.
Some are fleeing the version of themselves their Stratum assigned them.

They should feel like pressure made visible.

### Core fantasy
Runaways embody:
- fracture
- defection
- exile
- narrative instability
- guilt
- reinvention
- the cost of social order

### Public function
In authored content, Runaways provide:
- chase hooks
- moral choices
- revelation triggers
- faction conflict sparks
- recruitment opportunities
- Turned Confidant seeds

### Hidden function
Runaways also:
- expose contradictions between official and private truth
- reveal what each Strain and Stratum punishes
- carry embarrassing records out of sealed systems
- act as mobile Buried Clues
- pressure the player to choose between order, mercy, usefulness, and truth

### Runaway subtype library

#### Defectors
Political breakaways who reject a Clique, Stratum authority, or strain-aligned doctrine.

Use them for:
- Clique conflict
- Great Dispute foreshadowing
- Crownroot / Brassroot tension
- social-choice branching

#### Breakaways
Escaped criminals or labor fugitives who may be guilty, framed, or inconveniently both.

Use them for:
- pursuit content
- Wild Posts
- sympathy vs discipline choices
- lighter-stakes recurring chase content

#### Dissidents
Vanished nobles or polished social figures who rejected their assigned place.

Use them for:
- Buried Court content
- Court Steward / High Confidant intrigue
- elegant scandal
- controlled-image collapse

#### Loose Quills
Researchers, archivists, and record-tamperers who know too much or published the wrong thing.

Use them for:
- Ledgerhollow investigations
- Lore Ledger content
- Confidence Trail progression
- Deepening setup

#### Turncoats
Former military, enforcers, or quiet functionaries who changed sides and now know how force actually works.

Use them for:
- combat-adjacent social content
- Wild Ones overlap
- post-boss fallout
- recruitment into harder systems

#### Gonefolk
The half-mythic category.
People who disappeared from one version of the story and reappeared in another.

Use them for:
- Memory Shift foreshadowing
- Hushline deep content
- surreal late-game Duties
- causality unease

### Writing rules for Runaways
Runaways should rarely be written as purely innocent or purely villainous.
Their best use is tension.

Good Runaway writing should make the player ask:
- what are they escaping?
- who benefits from calling them a runaway?
- are they telling the truth, the usable truth, or a truth they can survive with?

### Runaway reward profile
Common rewards:
- Trust with specific Confidants
- Buried Clue progress
- Standing shifts
- Strata Seals in later layers
- rare access to hidden posts or alternate encounter lines

### Best Strata overlap
- **Ledgerhollow**: Loose Quills, record leaks, investigation branches
- **Wayworks**: movement, pursuit, escort, rerouting
- **The Buried Court**: Dissidents, social disgrace, curated truth
- **Memory Fen**: Gonefolk, impossible memories, ritual fugitives
- **Loamwake**: accessible moral-intro version of the system

## The Wild Ones

### Identity
**The Wild Ones** are the rough pressure edge of the world.
They may be raiders, hunters, off-book enforcers, feral opportunists, frontier specialists, or a mix depending on local context.

They should feel like a category that respectable society pretends is external even when it clearly feeds on them.

### Core fantasy
The Wild Ones embody:
- predation
- deniable violence
- frontier appetite
- pressure without paperwork
- rough movement through unsafe spaces
- the social underside of order

### Public function
In authored content, The Wild Ones provide:
- threat escalation
- travel tension
- Wild Posts
- pursuit content
- combat excuses with social context
- dangerous intermediaries between civilized and uncivilized story frames

### Hidden function
The Wild Ones also:
- do work polite factions deny needing done
- reveal where power gets enforced off the books
- turn routes into politics
- expose who funds instability while condemning it
- convert fear into local order

### Wild Ones subtype library

#### Trackers
Quiet route readers and pursuit specialists.

Use them for:
- chases
- evidence trails
- escort inversion content
- Wayworks pressure

#### Rakers
Rough raiders, scavengers, and hard-entry opportunists.

Use them for:
- resource theft Duties
- disruption events
- salvage contention
- frontier comedy with danger

#### Chasers
Open pursuit specialists.
Fast, direct, and harder to bargain with.

Use them for:
- Breakaway pursuit chains
- escalating Duties
- danger spikes during travel

#### Fangs
Elite agents or especially feared field operators.

Use them for:
- post-Deepening pressure
- Buried Court content
- anti-player intimidation scenes
- deniable-state violence

#### Rousers
Agitators, noise-makers, rumor-setters, and chaotic social accelerants.

Use them for:
- Clique disruption
- false flags
- crowd incidents
- comic menace with real consequences

### Writing rules for The Wild Ones
The Wild Ones should not be reduced to generic bandits.
They should feel like a social category with local histories, practical logic, and ugly usefulness.

Good Wild Ones scenes should suggest:
- someone profits from this
- someone is pretending not to know that
- violence here is both real and theatrically managed

### Wild Ones reward profile
Common rewards:
- combat rewards
- Salvage materials
- Crack Coins
- Burrowfolk training hooks
- Standing shifts with harder-aligned factions
- chase resolution rewards

### Best Strata overlap
- **Wayworks**: routes, ambushes, pursuit logic
- **Loamwake**: safe introduction to roughness
- **The Buried Court**: elite-backed violence and deniable pressure
- **Memory Fen**: strange ferality, ritual corruption, misdirected pursuit
- **Ledgerhollow**: information theft and courier interception

## Family intersections

### Wanderers and Runaways
Useful for:
- mistaken identity
- shelter choices
- “escort or expose” Duties
- hidden recruitment lines

### Wanderers and The Wild Ones
Useful for:
- toll routes
- extortion
- protection deals
- route-control storylines

### Runaways and The Wild Ones
Useful for:
- chase loops
- bounty-style pressure without explicit bounty framing
- Turned Hand origin stories
- morally compromised rescue chains

### All three together
Use sparingly.
When all three appear in one authored sequence, the player should feel they have stumbled into a crossroads where:
- truth is moving
- danger is following
- commerce is adapting

Those should be high-energy scenarios.

## Relationship to Confidants and Cliques

### Confidants
Each roaming family should feed different Confidant role stories:
- **Archivist** ↔ Loose Quills, Messengers, Buried Clues
- **Fixer** ↔ Scrappers, Rakers, repair disputes
- **High Confidant** ↔ Dissidents, Messengers, Fangs
- **Pathfinder** ↔ Peddlers, Trackers, escort routes
- **Witness** ↔ Pilgrims, Gonefolk, Smoke Whispers
- **Broker** ↔ protection deals, route exchanges, defectors
- **Turned Hand** ↔ Breakaways, Turncoats, Chasers

### Cliques
Cliques should respond differently to each family.

Example patterns:
- Helpful local Cliques tolerate Wanderers but distrust Runaways
- Record Cliques seek Messengers and Loose Quills but fear Gonefolk
- Court Cliques exploit Wanderers, conceal Dissidents, and hire around Wild Ones
- Labor Cliques clash with Rakers but shelter useful Breakaways
- Quiet Cliques treat Pilgrims and Gonefolk as omens or evidence

## Deepening use

### Before First Deepening
Use these families to make the world feel active and textured.
Keep revelations light.

### First–Second Deepening
Start showing that movement between Strata is also movement between versions of the truth.

### Third Deepening
Runaways and Wanderers should begin carrying contradictory records, impossible memories, and route knowledge that should not exist.

### Fourth Deepening
The Wild Ones should become legibly entangled with respectable power.
Gonefolk and Oddfolk should no longer feel purely incidental.

### Fifth Deepening
These families should help reveal that the Buried Court did not just fail to prevent collapse.
It may have structured how collapse continued to circulate.

## Production templates

### Wanderer template
- subtype
- surface need
- hidden motive
- delivery surface
- system reward
- one contradiction
- one memorable line

### Runaway template
- subtype
- accusation against them
- what they say really happened
- who wants them found
- player choice pressure
- consequence branch

### Wild One template
- subtype
- local threat pattern
- who unofficially benefits
- combat/non-combat path
- reward profile
- aftermath state

---

## Tone rules

### Do
- let humor and unease coexist
- make people sound socially specific
- use movement as a source of story truth
- let classes blur at the edges
- keep political satire grounded in behavior, not speeches

### Avoid
- generic fantasy traveler writing
- pure morality labels
- random bandit energy without context
- making every roaming NPC a twist reveal
- flattening all three families into “just encounter tables”
