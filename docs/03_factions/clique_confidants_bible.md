# Gnome Game — Clique + Confidants Bible

## Purpose
This document defines the social and relationship layer of the project: **Cliques**, **Confidants**, associated **Burrowfolk** patterns, and the tone of social progression inside the Burrow and across the Strata.

It is a production guide for:
- social system design
- UI copy
- post/notices writing
- Confidant unlocks and progression
- Burrowfolk unlock patterns
- faction flavor and social tension

It assumes the current canon from the master glossary, Phase 1 Content Bible, and Strains Bible.

---

## Core social rule
A **Clique** is not just a guild.

A Clique is a mixed social-political-survival circle built around:
- practical support
- shared ambition
- mutual leverage
- protection inside unstable society
- collective access to resources, reputation, and Burrow opportunities

Cliques should feel like a mix of:
- neighborhood machine
- private club
- survival pact
- political cell
- friend group that may absolutely betray you later

That tension is the point.

---

## Clique fantasy
In a world where the Strata are fractured and everyone pretends collapse is normal, most gnomes cannot survive by themselves for long.

A Clique offers:
- resource pooling
- soft protection
- social cover
- standing boosts
- easier access to Burrow systems
- prestige by association

A bad Clique can also mean:
- petty hierarchy
- manipulation
- debt culture
- exclusion
- quiet coercion
- power blocs disguised as friendship

Cliques should therefore always read as useful, funny, and a little dangerous.

## Clique structure

### Founder
The founder is the first face of the Clique.

They should feel like:
- organizer
- ringmaster
- patron
- local power broker
- person who says “we’re just helping each other” while clearly building influence

### Steward
Stewards do the practical work.

They should feel like:
- moderators
- quartermasters
- event organizers
- gatekeepers
- quietly overworked managers who actually keep the Clique alive

### Burrowmate
Burrowmates are the body of the Clique.

They should feel like:
- regular members
- voters without formal vote language
- gossip carriers
- helpers, lurkers, contributors, and opportunists all at once

---

## Clique tone in writing
Clique writing should sound like:
- warm on the surface
- slightly transactional underneath
- playful and local
- political without using modern heavy jargon too often
- full of favors, nudges, little resentments, and coded respect

Good tone examples:
- “The Clique could use your hands.”
- “A Steward has posted a new Queue item.”
- “Favor moves quickly when noticed slowly.”
- “Burrowmates remember who gathered and who watched.”

---

## Clique pillars
Every Clique should be built from five visible pillars.

### 1. Mutual help
The public story.
Cliques present themselves as practical communities that keep gnomes fed, informed, and connected.

### 2. Standing
The social story.
Cliques increase access, status, and relevance.

### 3. Favors
The economic story.
Cliques convert time, action, and attention into Favor Marks and other opportunity.

### 4. Identity
The political story.
Cliques attract certain Strains, aesthetics, and worldviews.

### 5. Pressure
The hidden story.
Cliques can quietly demand loyalty, performance, and ideological alignment.

---

## Clique archetypes
Use these as reusable templates for content, not rigid classes.

### 1. The Helpful Local Clique
Public image:
- soup, repair, logistics, little acts of aid

Hidden issue:
- they know too much about everyone because they are always “helping”

Best Strain overlap:
- Freeroot
- Brassroot

### 2. The Record Clique
Public image:
- useful archivists, fact-finders, map sharers

Hidden issue:
- selective truth, gatekept access, quiet superiority

Best Strain overlap:
- Inkroot
- Hushline

### 3. The Court Clique
Public image:
- elegant hosts, polished organizers, culture keepers

Hidden issue:
- status obsession, soft exclusion, image control

Best Strain overlap:
- Crownroot

### 4. The Labor Clique
Public image:
- real workers, real output, no nonsense

Hidden issue:
- factional rigidity, rumor pipelines, “trusted source” dogma

Best Strain overlap:
- Brassroot

### 5. The Quiet Clique
Public image:
- harmless little old network, ritual keepers, memory tenders

Hidden issue:
- knows too much, remembers too much, may be preserving old power lines

Best Strain overlap:
- Hushline
- Crownroot

## Clique Queue design
The **Clique Queue** should never feel like generic chores.
It should feel like a mix of:
- practical communal tasks
- political signaling
- reputation farming
- soft commitment tests

Good Queue item categories:
- gather supplies
- answer a post
- escort a Wanderer
- inspect a Strata rumor
- contribute to a communal project
- assist a Steward
- mediate a dispute
- prepare for a Great Dispute phase

Queue items should reveal what the Clique values.

---

## Clique Call design
**Clique Call** is not just chat.
It is also:
- tone setting
- gossip channel
- pressure valve
- public memory
- community theater

Content types inside Clique Call:
- practical requests
- rumor sharing
- congratulations and public praise
- subtle shaming
- event coordination
- panic during crisis windows
- local jokes and repeated sayings

Writing note:
Clique Call should often carry more truth than official systems.

---

## Favor Marks philosophy
**Favor Marks** should feel like social credit without becoming sterile or dystopian in tone.

They represent:
- remembered contribution
- visible reliability
- social access
- repayable usefulness
- political goodwill

They should not feel like pure money.
They should feel like a quantified favor economy.

## Confidants — core rule
A **Confidant** is not just a companion.

A Confidant is any named relationship that can matter to the Gnome Survivor as:
- ally
- specialist
- advisor
- witness
- political asset
- strange friend
- turned foe

Confidants are where the game becomes personal.
The world can be absurd, but Confidants are how consequences stick.

## What a Confidant should always provide
Each Confidant should provide at least four things.

### 1. Mechanical identity
A clear Calling with gameplay effect.

### 2. Narrative tension
A reason they are not simply “good” or “useful.”

### 3. Social angle
A relationship to a Strain, Clique, or Stratum power structure.

### 4. Burrowfolk tie
A Burrowfolk class or theme they naturally unlock, train, or influence.

---

## Confidant relationship model
A Confidant relationship should progress like this:

1. **Clue Trigger**
2. first appearance / Post / Encounter
3. partial cooperation
4. mission or moral complication
5. Confidence Trail escalation
6. formal unlock
7. Trust progression through Posts, tasks, and events

This ensures Confidants feel discovered, not dispensed.

---

## Confidant role archetypes

### Archivist
Best for:
- Inkroot
- classified truth
- Ledgerhollow
- map, record, clue progression

Typical conflict:
They know something real and dangerous but cannot decide whether sharing it helps or destabilizes everyone.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Keepers
- Fixers

### Fixer
Best for:
- Brassroot
- systems repair
- Salvage Shed
- practical output and upgrades

Typical conflict:
They can solve almost anything except the question of who benefits from what they build.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Hands
- Fixers
- Bruisers

### High Confidant
Best for:
- Crownroot
- old prestige systems
- elite access
- hidden authority structures

Typical conflict:
They are polite proof that the old world still thinks it owns the new one.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Stewards
- Keepers

### Pathfinder
Best for:
- Freeroot
- Loamwake
- travel loops
- Strata discovery

Typical conflict:
They know every route out, but no route is morally clean.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Scouts
- Hands

### Witness
Best for:
- Hushline
- Memory Fen
- Echo systems
- ritual truth and selective memory

Typical conflict:
They have seen too much and believe silence may still be a duty.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Chantfolk
- Keepers

### Broker
Best for:
- Clique systems
- political favors
- Burrow Post events
- Wanderer and Runaway handling

Typical conflict:
They are useful because they can make anything move, which means they are never clean.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Stewards
- Hands

### Turned Hand
Best for:
- reclaimed foes
- Wild Ones defectors
- Warden survivors
- morally sharp unlocks

Typical conflict:
They did real harm before they changed sides.
Trust should feel earned, never automatic.

Burrowfolk tie:
- Bruisers
- Scouts
- Fixers depending on origin

## Burrowfolk relationship to Confidants
Each Confidant should shape the Burrowfolk layer in a specific way.

### Default pattern
A Confidant should do one or more of the following:
- unlock a Burrowfolk class
- improve training speed
- open a specialized Burrow Work category
- alter Command bonuses in Clash
- modify non-combat utility inside a specific Stratum

### Burrowfolk classes already locked
- Hands
- Scouts
- Bruisers
- Fixers
- Keepers
- Chantfolk
- Stewards

### Example pairings
- Archivist → Keepers
- Fixer → Fixers
- High Confidant → Stewards
- Pathfinder → Scouts
- Witness → Chantfolk
- Broker → Hands or Stewards
- Turned Hand → Bruisers or Scouts

## Confidant Posts design
**Confidant Posts** should be one of the strongest tone carriers in the game.

They can contain:
- requests
- confessions
- updates
- rumors
- bait
- emotional turns
- hidden clues
- contradictions between public and private self

Writing rule:
A Confidant Post should sound like a person, not a quest giver.

Good Confidant Post functions:
- deepen Trust
- reveal faction politics
- unlock a Duty
- trigger a Buried Clue
- complicate a prior choice
- hint at Deepening truth

---

## Turned Confidants
Turned Confidants deserve special handling.
They should never feel like “enemy but now friendly pet.”

They should feel like:
- uneasy allies
- compromised survivors
- coerced defectors
- people trying to outlive a terrible past

Good sources for Turned Confidants:
- a Wild One Fang who breaks ranks
- a Claimant who loses status but keeps secrets
- a Warden subordinate who realizes the system is rotten
- a rogue researcher who crossed a line and lived with it

Trust progression for Turned Confidants should be harder, slower, and more morally layered.

---

## Confidant design rules by Strain

### Freeroot Confidants
Should feel:
- warm
- observant
- evasive in subtle ways
- better at survival than doctrine

### Inkroot Confidants
Should feel:
- precise
- unsettlingly informed
- prone to classifying people too fast
- torn between truth and control

### Brassroot Confidants
Should feel:
- practical
- blunt
- productive
- dangerous when certainty hardens into ideology

### Crownroot Confidants
Should feel:
- polished
- entitled
- strategic
- emotionally trained for hierarchy

### Hushline Confidants
Should feel:
- quiet
- ritualized
- older than they should seem
- aware of memory as a weapon

---

## Confidant count guidance
For early production, target:
- **1 flagship Confidant per Strain**
- **1 additional mixed-allegiance Confidant per two Strata**
- **1 Turned Confidant** in the first full playable arc

This keeps the cast readable while still allowing political overlap.

---

## Clique × Confidant crossover rules
Confidants and Cliques should overlap, but not collapse into one another.

Good patterns:
- a Confidant belongs to a Clique but is not defined only by it
- a Clique protects one Confidant and distrusts another
- Clique Queue items can strengthen or harm Trust with a Confidant
- Confidant Posts can create friction inside Clique Call

This is where your political satire can breathe.

---

## Production checklist
When designing a new Clique, define:
- public image
- hidden pressure point
- dominant Strain presence
- what it wants from the player
- what it quietly demands from members

When designing a new Confidant, define:
- role tag
- Calling
- Strain tie
- Stratum tie
- Burrowfolk tie
- Clue Trigger
- first Trust complication
- Deepening relevance

---

## Recommended early implementation priority
1. one starter Clique in **Loamwake**
2. one more status-heavy Clique in **Ledgerhollow**
3. 1 Confidant each for Freeroot, Inkroot, Brassroot
4. 1 High Confidant in the early mid-game
5. 1 Witness in Memory Fen
6. first Turned Confidant after first serious Warden arc

That gives you social breadth without exploding scope.
