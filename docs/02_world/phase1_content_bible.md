# Gnome Game — Phase 1 Content Bible

## Scope
This document starts the **Phase 1 Content Bible** for the setting. It builds on the locked naming from the master glossary and focuses first on the **5 launch Strata**.

This is not a final quest script. It is a **worldbuilding and production guide** for story, art, encounter design, rewards, enemies, Confidants, and progression hooks.

---

## Core Story Spine

Gnome civilization was once a polished underground empire stitched together from:
- elegant burrow-courts
- absurd bureaucracy
- tinkerer city-state networks
- aristocratic prestige culture

Its downfall began when the ruling classes betrayed the rest of gnome society. That betrayal drove geopolitical unrest, sped up reckless invention, and pushed the old world into war and machine catastrophe.

The modern world looks calm at first glance. It is not. The Strata are isolated region-states pretending everything is normal while living inside the ruins of a broken civilization.

The five Strains are not just biology. Depending on who is speaking, they are seen as:
- honored bloodlines
- dangerous mutations
- survival adaptations
- political castes
- spiritual signs

**Deepening** reveals buried truth. Each Deepening pulls the Gnome Survivor further into old history, lost authority, hidden memory, and the long aftermath of the betrayal.

---

## Design Rules For All 5 Strata

Each Stratum should answer five questions:
1. **What kind of gnomes dominate here?**
2. **What lie does this region tell about the old world?**
3. **What survival strategy won here after the collapse?**
4. **What does the player farm here?**
5. **What truth gets uncovered here during Deepening?**

Every Stratum should have:
- one dominant Strain identity
- one strong visual metaphor
- one obvious everyday routine
- one hidden social rot
- one major Warden
- one Confidant unlock path
- one Buried Clue chain
- one reward identity tied to gameplay

# The 5 Launch Strata

## 1. Loamwake
### Role in progression
Starter Stratum. Introduces survival, simple faction politics, first real Clash loops, first Confidant, first Burrowfolk class, and the idea that the world is still damaged under the surface.

### Dominant Strain
**Freeroot**

### Public face
Loamwake presents itself as a peaceful, earthy, easygoing frontier. The gnomes brew, forage, explore, trade, and insist that everyone should relax and coexist.

### Hidden truth
Loamwake only looks peaceful because it pushes conflict to the edges. It survives by letting bolder, harsher groups fight elsewhere while it scavenges stability from the margins. Its kindness is real, but so is its avoidance.

### Survival philosophy
**Adapt through softness. Endure by bending.**

### Visual tone
- damp earth
- glowing mushrooms
- patched fences
- hanging lantern jars
- old military scrap reused as cozy decor
- trails, rope bridges, carts, root tunnels

### Everyday life
- brewing
- fungus tending
- low-stakes barter
- trail gossip
- scavenged tool repair
- exploratory wandering disguised as leisure

### Mechanical identity
- onboarding
- battle basics
- first resource loops
- first Strain exposure
- first exploration pacing lessons
- light emphasis on survivability and gathering

### Farm identity
- basic resources
- first Mushcaps surplus
- entry treasure pieces
- simple Clothes upgrade materials
- early Strain Seeds

### Main enemies
- territorial scavengers
- feral burrow beasts
- abandoned defense scraps still active in the soil
- small Wild Ones crews testing the edges of the settlement

### Burrowfolk focus
**Hands** and **Scouts**

### Confidant shape
A Freeroot trail-guide or brewer-explorer who seems unserious at first, but quietly understands how people survive by refusing to harden.

### Warden concept
A frontier protector who once chose compromise over direct resistance and now embodies Loamwake’s central flaw: peace without accountability.

### Buried Clue theme
The first signs that the fall of civilization was not simply “old machines gone wrong.” Somebody made choices.

### Deepening reveal
Loamwake was not ignored during the fall. It was used as a buffer zone.

### Good production anchors
- “cozy decay”
- “festival camp built on a trench line”
- “earthy but politically evasive”

## 2. Ledgerhollow
### Role in progression
Second Stratum. Introduces harder clue chains, archive systems, stronger resource targeting, and the feeling that information itself is a power class.

### Dominant Strain
**Inkroot**

### Public face
Ledgerhollow claims to be the home of facts, records, maps, and proper order. It presents itself as the grown-up Stratum: methodical, informed, and above emotional nonsense.

### Hidden truth
Ledgerhollow hoards truth the way others hoard food. Its obsession with records is not neutral. It filters, edits, ranks, buries, and withholds. It survives by deciding which version of reality becomes official.

### Survival philosophy
**Control memory, control power.**

### Visual tone
- stacked ledgers
- filing walls
- numbered vault halls
- map tables
- old stamp-seals and brass indexing systems
- scholars with field gear and dust masks

### Everyday life
- record copying
- evidence sorting
- survey debate
- archive salvage
- map revision
- official notice distribution

### Mechanical identity
- stronger treasure targeting
- archive-related systems
- card exchange focus
- deeper Clue Trigger chains
- more noticeable stat gates

### Farm identity
- fragments and treasure materials
- information-heavy encounter rewards
- archive-targeted progression items
- stronger research inputs

### Main enemies
- hostile claim-jumpers in old record vaults
- archive vermin
- memory-corrupted machines that still categorize living things as inventory
- dissident Inkroot splinter cells

### Burrowfolk focus
**Keepers** and **Fixers**

### Confidant shape
An Archivist who believes facts can save the world, but has to confront the difference between truth and official truth.

### Warden concept
A perfect record-keeper who values continuity over justice and would rather preserve a lie than destabilize the archive.

### Buried Clue theme
Evidence that major pieces of the old war narrative were rewritten.

### Deepening reveal
The betrayal was documented. It was simply classified.

### Good production anchors
- “civilization as paperwork fortress”
- “truth buried under neat shelves”
- “obsessive record culture with soft authoritarian edges”

---

## 3. Memory Fen
### Role in progression
Third Stratum. Deepens ritual systems, Echo economy, hidden systems, and the first stronger feeling that Deepening is connecting memory, myth, and usable power.

### Dominant Strain
**Hushline** with pressure from **Crownroot** traditions

### Public face
Memory Fen is hushed, ceremonial, and old. It presents itself as a keeper of tradition, ancestral respect, quiet observance, and sacred continuity.

### Hidden truth
Memory Fen does not just remember the dead. It edits what is allowed to be remembered. Reverence and censorship are entangled here. The quiet is not peace. It is control.

### Survival philosophy
**Preserve identity through ritual.**

### Visual tone
- wet stone shrines
- reeds, mist, candles, bone-white markers
- echo pools
- ancestor iconography
- ceremonial paths
- half-flooded remembrance halls

### Everyday life
- offerings
- recitations
- memorial tending
- silence customs
- ritual witness duties
- interpretation of inherited signs

### Mechanical identity
- Echo loops
- Ancestral Depictor focus
- Smoke Whispers hidden events
- stronger story resonance
- more layered Buried Clues

### Farm identity
- Echoes
- Echo Shards
- Elder Book-related ritual progression
- memory-linked treasure materials

### Main enemies
- memory-warped guardians
- drowned shrine machines
- fanatical ritual enforcers
- Whisper-haunted remnants that react to Gnome Pipe triggers

### Burrowfolk focus
**Chantfolk** and **Keepers**

### Confidant shape
A Witness or ritual keeper who knows far more than they say and may already suspect earlier Deepenings.

### Warden concept
A ceremonial authority figure who believes forgetting is sometimes necessary for survival and has built an entire moral framework around selective remembrance.

### Buried Clue theme
The old civilization created remembrance systems that could outlive death, rank, and even a single timeline of events.

### Deepening reveal
Some Hushline figures may retain fragments across cycles.

### Good production anchors
- “holy swamp archive”
- “grief as policy”
- “ritual beauty covering historical suppression”

## 4. Wayworks
### Role in progression
Fourth Stratum. Focuses on travel efficiency, expansion logic, infrastructure, throughput, expedition tempo, and the practical machinery of keeping civilization moving.

### Dominant Strain
**Brassroot**

### Public face
Wayworks is the Stratum of motion, labor, connection, and results. It sees itself as the only honest place left: routes, tools, freight, engineering, and real work.

### Hidden truth
Wayworks confuses output with righteousness. It values function so hard that it excuses coercion, propaganda, and cruelty as necessary efficiency.

### Survival philosophy
**Build first. Justify later.**

### Visual tone
- rails, winches, lifts, conveyors, foundry glow
- patched engines
- route markers
- loading bays
- maintenance tunnels
- industrial uniforms made from old prestige fabrics

### Everyday life
- route scheduling
- repair shifts
- hauling
- shift whistles
- logistics arguments
- workshop politics

### Mechanical identity
- exploration tempo
- travel speed
- route optimization
- stronger industrial resource loops
- build planning

### Farm identity
- reinforcement materials
- higher-output Burrow upgrade resources
- route-based expedition rewards
- materials for later stronger Clothes progression

### Main enemies
- automated route guardians
- labor gangs gone rogue
- overloaded scrap hulks
- Wild Ones splinter crews who grew out of abandoned security labor

### Burrowfolk focus
**Fixers** and **Bruisers**

### Confidant shape
A builder-operator or Fixer who genuinely keeps people alive, but has helped maintain a brutal system because somebody had to keep the lines moving.

### Warden concept
A logistics tyrant who sees every living thing as part of a throughput equation.

### Buried Clue theme
The machine catastrophe was not accidental in the simple sense. It was incentivized.

### Deepening reveal
The war economy did not merely happen after the betrayal. It was one of its tools.

### Good production anchors
- “industrial tunnel-city”
- “labor pride with authoritarian drift”
- “grim utility wrapped in practical humor”

## 5. The Buried Court
### Role in progression
Fifth Stratum and pre-Deepening convergence zone. The player’s understanding of the world should stop feeling regional here and start feeling civilizational.

### Dominant Strain
**Crownroot** under pressure from all others

### Public face
The Buried Court is the dream of restored legitimacy: old rank, polished authority, ceremony, governance, bloodline, and the promise that proper rule can make the world whole again.

### Hidden truth
The Buried Court sits closest to the original betrayal. It is not simply the victim of collapse. It helped author the moral language that justified it.

### Survival philosophy
**Order is destiny. Authority restores the world.**

### Visual tone
- buried palaces
- collapsed audience halls
- sealed lifts
- ceremonial banners in ruin
- cracked portraits
- court fashion mixed with survival patchwork
- old command architecture still imposing through debris

### Everyday life
- rank rituals
- title disputes
- restoration politics
- seal claims
- legacy enforcement
- selective hospitality

### Mechanical identity
- multi-system optimization
- strongest culture/stat thresholds before Deepening
- Confidant and Strain convergence
- strongest political story beats before cycle shift

### Farm identity
- high-tier treasures
- Deepening-gate materials
- Crownroot-related unlocks
- late-cycle milestone resources

### Main enemies
- elite court remnants
- sealed palace guardians
- old authority constructs
- manipulated Claimants competing for the right to narrate the past

### Burrowfolk focus
**Stewards** and **Bruisers**

### Confidant shape
A High Confidant, Broker, or Court-linked figure forced to decide whether restoration means justice or simply reinstallation.

### Warden concept
A ruler-survivor who believes they alone can carry the memory and legitimacy of civilization, regardless of cost.

### Buried Clue theme
The betrayal was a policy choice, not a panic response.

### Deepening reveal
The old world may not be gone in the way everyone assumes. Parts of it may still be waiting beneath the official ending.

### Good production anchors
- “fallen court under the dirt”
- “restoration fantasy rotting from inside”
- “the beauty and violence of legitimacy”

# Cross-Strata Production Notes

## Strain pressure map
- **Loamwake** → Freeroot dominance
- **Ledgerhollow** → Inkroot dominance
- **Memory Fen** → Hushline dominance with Crownroot ritual influence
- **Wayworks** → Brassroot dominance
- **The Buried Court** → Crownroot dominance with all others orbiting its legacy

## Story escalation
- **Loamwake** asks: what happened here?
- **Ledgerhollow** asks: who controlled the record?
- **Memory Fen** asks: what was allowed to be remembered?
- **Wayworks** asks: who profited from the machine order?
- **The Buried Court** asks: who chose the betrayal, and why?

## Tone rule
No Stratum should ever be one-note grim.
Every region needs:
- one absurd social habit
- one cozy visual surface
- one petty political behavior
- one real wound underneath it all

## UI and writing rule
Use the locked glossary consistently:
- no swapping between “Relics” and “Treasures”
- no swapping between “Gene Lab” and “Strain Hall”
- no swapping between “Prestige” and “Deepening”
