# Core Loop MVP Spec

**Status:** Canon — Phase 2A Salvage Rewrite  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Date:** 2026-04-19  
**Scope:** Loamwake-only MVP loop. First 10 minutes, first 60 minutes, first daily return, proof-of-fun criteria.

---

## Status, Scope, and MVP Goal

**MVP = Burrow + Loamwake only.**

The goal is to demonstrate: _"A tiny gnome survivor managing their underground home while exploring a broken post-fallout stratum, building their first gear, and uncovering the bones of a forgotten train."_

This spec describes what the player does and feels during their first session, not what the full game eventually becomes.

All other Strata, full Rootrail networks, War Armory, Memory Shift, full Deepening, Crack runs, and full event cycles are out of scope for the prototype build.

## Player Fantasy Proven by MVP

The MVP must validate these three pillars:

1. **"My burrow is alive."** — The Burrow produces resources while I'm away. I have a Burrowfolk (placeholder) working. I check in and collect.
2. **"Loamwake is interesting one screen at a time."** — Exploration drips discoveries (Wanderers, Buried Clues, resource nodes). Not overwhelming. Each explore tap has a chance of something new.
3. **"Building my first Fixture felt earned."** — The first Fixture crafting moment (cap 0→1 tutorial → craft → equip) creates a small but real sense of character growth.

## First Daily Return

**Goal:** Player comes back after 8+ hours and has things to collect and decisions to make.

On return, the player should find:
- Burrowfolk output ready to collect (8 hours of Tangled Root Twine / Crumbled Ore from deployed units)
- Daily Duties refreshed (2–3 simple gather + one story Duty available)
- Burrow Posts: 1–2 new posts available
- Rootrail: if they had enough Rootrail Parts, they can start the step 1 timer (8-hour timer for step 1)

**Decision on return:** "Do I spend my Mushcaps on exploring Mudpipe Hollow again to farm Ore for the second Fixture, or do I explore Rootvine Shelf for Root Twine to finish the Burrow Post?"

The first meaningful decision is resource allocation across parallel goals on day 1–2.

---

## First Meaningful Decision

**Timing:** By day 1–2 of play.

**Decision:** The player has limited Mushcaps. They can use them to:
- Continue farming materials to craft their third Fixture slot (Pure Attack build)
- Complete the daily Duty chain for a guaranteed Mooncap reward
- Explore deeper in Mudpipe Hollow to find the Buried Clue holding the Forgotten Manual (Rootrail step 1 gate)

There is no wrong answer. All three paths progress the player. The tension between farming, story, and the Rootrail project is the first meaningful choice and a preview of the game's core loop tension.

## First Fixture Acquisition

The first Fixture must be a **scripted, tutorial-guided experience** that teaches the no-slots model:

1. Tutorial text: *"You can carry tools that change how you work. You have no fixed harness — just bring what fits."*
2. Fixture cap increases from 0→1 (the player sees the counter change: `0/0` → `0/1`).
3. The crafting recipe is immediately presented with materials the player already has from their first explore.
4. Player crafts and equips. Counter becomes `1/1`.
5. A stat tooltip shows the active bonus clearly.

This sequence must happen within the first 5 minutes and must not be skippable.

## Early Fixture Crafting Loop

Post-tutorial, the crafting loop should feel like:
- Explore to gather materials → check recipe → craft → equip or hold for later
- No cooldown on crafting (instant or near-instant for early recipes)
- No premium gate on any of the first 5 recipes

**Early recipe pool (Loamwake, pre-Rootrail gate):**

## First Hat Unlock or Hat Tease

**Timing:** After completing the first Warden clear (The Mudgrip in Mudpipe Hollow).

**Beat:**
1. First Warden clear reward packet includes: resources + **Loamwake Dirt Cap** (hat).
2. Brief Hat Collection screen introduction: *"Every hat you unlock stays with you. Its spirit lingers even when you choose another."*
3. Player sets the Dirt Cap as their display hat.
4. +1% Mushcap gather rate passive activates (shown in passive summary).

The hat unlock does NOT show a full hat collection screen (scope limited). Just the unlock notification and the display selector.

---

## First Rootrail Reveal

**Timing:** Around 50–55 minutes in (end of Mudpipe Hollow Duty chain), or triggered by Greta's second Duty.

**Beat:**
1. Exploring zone_lw_002 (Mudpipe Hollow border) triggers a discovery event: rusted rail tracks.
2. Greta says (one line): *"Old track. Goes underground. Someone tried to hold this together, once."*
3. Rootrail Station tab appears in The Burrow navigation.
4. Player visits. Sees: "Rootrail Terminal — Loamwake Station." One repair step shown.
5. Step 1 requirements: 20 Rootrail Parts + Forgotten Manual (fman_001_terminal_routing).
6. Rootrail Parts count shown in wallet (player likely has 0–5 from exploration drops).
7. Forgotten Manual shown as NOT IN CODEX with a hint: *"Something in the Rootvine Shelf might lead to this."*
8. Player leaves knowing what to find next.

**What the player does NOT see at this point:** future routes, route silhouettes (one silhouette ok), full repair chain, repair costs for steps 2+.

## Proof-of-Fun Criteria

The MVP build passes if **all** of the following are observable in a 60-minute play session with a new account:

| Criterion | Validation Method |
|-----------|------------------|
| Player crafts and equips at least one Fixture without confusion | Observation / UX test |
| Player returns after 8 hours and has meaningful things to collect | Session logging |
| Player can articulate what the Rootrail is and what they need next | Post-session interview |
| Player attempts at least 2 different Duty types in first session | Analytics event check |
| No single Mushcap spend feels pointless (every explore result gives something) | Content review |
| Player does not ask "what am I supposed to do?" after minute 10 | UX observation |
| Burrowfolk output collected at least once per session (retention signal) | Analytics event check |

---

## Failure Signals

If any of the following are observed, the MVP loop requires revision before content expansion:

- Player ignores the Fixture system after the tutorial
- Player never visits the Rootrail station in session 1 (Greta reveal not landing)
- Mushcap pacing causes "I ran out, nothing to do" within the first 30 minutes
- Burrowfolk output feels irrelevant (outputs are wrong resource type for current goals)
- Players do not return for a second session (day-1 retention below threshold)
