# Gnome Game — Burrow Posts Examples

## Purpose
This file turns the Post architecture into **example content packets**.

It is not a final narrative script doc.
It is a **production-facing sample library** showing how Burrow Posts, Confidant Posts, Stranger Posts, and Wild Posts can feel in practice.

This file is designed around a long live-service assumption:
- the game can eventually support **100+ named or semi-named roaming content sources**
- recurring characters should feel distinct without each needing a fully bespoke system
- old Posts should remain reusable through escalation, callback references, and Deepening-aware variants

This doc assumes canon from:
- `master_glossary.md`
- `phase1_content_bible.md`
- `strains_bible.md`
- `clique_confidants_bible.md`
- `deepening_arc.md`
- `duties_and_encounters.md`
- `wanderers_runaways_wild_ones.md`
- `burrow_posts.md`

---

## Example format
Each example includes:
- **Channel**
- **Source**
- **Headline**
- **Summary**
- **Duty**
- **Encounter shape**
- **Mechanical use**
- **Scaling notes**

# 1. Burrow Post examples

## 1.1 Dewpond complaint
**Channel:** Burrow Posts  
**Source:** Provision Board  
**Headline:** Dewpond Drawn Low Again  
**Summary:** Someone has been siphoning clean dew before morning count. The left basin is down to a sulking puddle and three Stewards are accusing each other in writing.  
**Duty:** Gather from Dewpond, inspect Tickworks timing, question one Burrowmate, repair one local production issue.  
**Encounter shape:** petty civic dispute that reveals minor sabotage or desperate household behavior.  
**Mechanical use:** early-game resource tutorial, production loop reminder, low-stakes humor.  
**Scaling notes:** later variants can reveal Clique influence, Runaway tampering, or Buried Clue traces hidden in repeated shortages.

## 1.2 Rootmine collapse notice
**Channel:** Burrow Posts  
**Source:** Burrow Box / Rootmine shift board  
**Headline:** Rootmine Tunnel Seven Refuses To Be Safe  
**Summary:** Tunnel Seven has become structurally opinionated. Workers insist it groans only when a Brassroot foreman lies.  
**Duty:** Send Burrowfolk, spend Burrow Rushes or materials, clear a short hazard Encounter.  
**Encounter shape:** repair + environmental joke + faction friction.  
**Mechanical use:** building-expansion sink, Burrowfolk work assignment, Brassroot flavor.  
**Scaling notes:** in later Deepenings, collapses can expose ancient conduits, sealed records, or low-tier Memory Shift leakage.

## 1.3 Tickworks backpay panic
**Channel:** Burrow Posts  
**Source:** Tickworks clerk  
**Headline:** Time Owed, Time Claimed, Time Miscounted  
**Summary:** Tickworks has issued three different output tallies and all of them insist they are the original.  
**Duty:** compare output logs, complete a Burrow Game, choose which record becomes official.  
**Encounter shape:** bureaucratic contradiction with playful consequence.  
**Mechanical use:** introduces storage/output systems and establishes the absurd technocratic tone.  
**Scaling notes:** repeated versions can become an ongoing side chain with a recurring clerk NPC.

## 1.4 Clique Queue spillover
**Channel:** Burrow Posts  
**Source:** Clique Call  
**Headline:** Your Clique Has Volunteered You Again  
**Summary:** A Steward has publicly offered your labor to settle a dispute involving ladders, hats, and interpretation of a shared wall.  
**Duty:** solve a social mini-task, spend or earn Favor Marks, pick a side.  
**Encounter shape:** social comedy with mild faction consequence.  
**Mechanical use:** introduces Clique identity and Standing gains/losses.  
**Scaling notes:** later versions can branch into Broker, High Confidant, or Wild Ones pressure.

## 1.5 Provision Board rewrite
**Channel:** Burrow Posts  
**Source:** Provision Board  
**Headline:** Socket Rules Revised After Incident  
**Summary:** New posting claims Treasures may no longer be Set into production sockets while glowing, humming, or offering legal advice.  
**Duty:** Set or remove one Treasure, reinforce one production node, complete a tutorial explanation step.  
**Encounter shape:** system-teaching via comedic incident report.  
**Mechanical use:** Treasure-to-production onboarding.  
**Scaling notes:** ideal reusable frame for any new system rule after launch.

# 6. Scaling rules for 100+ roaming content sources

## Rule 1 — characters should recur in roles, not only names
A Peddler should be able to:
- sell items
- bring rumors
- betray someone accidentally
- return later with history
- cross into event weeks

## Rule 2 — every new source should hook at least two systems
A new Runaway should touch at least two of:
- Burrow Posts
- Wild Posts
- Buried Clues
- Strata travel pressure
- Confidant Trust
- Clique Standing

## Rule 3 — difficulty should compound through overlap
Do not only increase health or numbers.
Increase difficulty by:
- overlapping timers
- conflicting trust demands
- route pressure
- post saturation
- false leads
- repeated faces with changed motives

## Rule 4 — returning characters should remember state
A reused named character should reflect:
- past help or betrayal
- prior Strata contact
- Deepening progression
- Confidence Trail state where relevant

## Rule 5 — not every recurring figure needs full biography
Create three layers:
- **named major recurring figures**
- **semi-named repeaters**
- **template repeaters with one twist**

This keeps scale manageable while still feeling populated.

---

# 7. Launch recommendation
At launch, aim for a content library shaped more like this:

## Burrow Posts
- 35–50 base templates
- 10–15 recurring civic chains

## Confidant Posts
- 8–12 per launch Confidant role family
- 3–5 longer Confidence Trail chains

## Stranger Posts
- 30–40 base templates
- 20+ named or semi-named Wanderer variants

## Wild Posts
- 20–30 base templates
- 10+ pursuit/escalation chains

This keeps launch content feeling busy and replayable while leaving plenty of room to scale toward a much larger future cast.

## Final production note
Do not treat this file as a script lock.
Treat it as a **content-pattern library**.

Writers and designers should be able to take one example here and generate:
- three safer early-game variants
- two harder mid-game variants
- one event-week variant
- one Deepening-aware callback
- one comedy version
- one unsettling version

That is how this system grows to live-service scale without becoming noise.
