# Content Scaling Rules

## Purpose

This document defines how **Gnome Survivor** scales its recurring content over months and years without collapsing under complexity. It is the canonical rulebook for expanding:

- **Wanderers**
- **Runaways**
- **The Wild Ones**
- **Burrow Posts**
- **Lucky Draw Week / Treasure Week / Echo Week**
- **Festival Ledger**
- **Deepening-era content layers**

It exists to solve one core live-service problem:

> The cast and content library must be able to grow well past **100 recurring roaming content sources** without making the game unreadable, unmaintainable, or impossible to balance.

## Core Scaling Philosophy

### Rule 1 — Add breadth before depth, then depth before novelty
Early expansion should mostly add:
- new Wanderers
- new Runaways
- new Wild Ones cells
- new Burrow Posts
- new event reward variants

Later expansion should increasingly add:
- relationship chains
- post-prestige encounter variants
- Strata-specific branching
- Confidant subpaths
- Deepening-aware remixes

This prevents the game from becoming “too many systems, not enough content” or “too many one-off gimmicks.”

### Rule 2 — New content must compound, not replace
Every major new content family must:
- connect to at least **2 existing systems**
- create at least **1 new loop**
- preserve relevance of older content

Example:
A new Wanderer should not just be a one-off NPC. It should potentially feed:
- Burrow Posts
- Duties
- Treasure targeting
- Confidant trust
- Clique Queue progress
- later Deepening reveals

### Rule 3 — Reuse content bricks aggressively
A scalable live-service game is not built from infinite handcrafted stories.
It is built from recombinable bricks.

Every scalable content source should be built from:
- **role**
- **strain association**
- **Strata association**
- **trigger condition**
- **reward lane**
- **escalation state**
- **post-completion recurrence rule**

### Rule 4 — Old content never becomes dead content
A content source may age out of the front page, but it should remain relevant through one or more of these:
- loot refresh
- Trust progression
- Deepening variant
- event bonus interaction
- Burrow Post trigger role
- faction progress tie-in
- collection completion

### Rule 5 — Readability matters more than raw volume
Players should feel like the world is becoming denser, not noisier.
A cast of 120+ recurring roamers is acceptable.
A cast of 120+ equally visible and equally urgent roamers is not.

## Scaling Model Overview

The game should scale on **four parallel axes**.

### Axis A — Roster Size
How many named or semi-named recurring content sources exist.

Examples:
- Wanderers
- Runaways
- Wild Ones crews
- Confidants
- Wardens

### Axis B — Interaction Depth
How many ways a content source can matter.

Examples:
- one-off reward
- repeated Burrow Posts
- chain encounters
- Trust progression
- event crossover
- Deepening reveal

### Axis C — System Reach
How many systems the content source touches.

Examples:
- economy
- combat
- Burrow production
- Clique progression
- Strain advancement
- Treasures

### Axis D — Historical Layering
How much old content changes after later Deepenings.

Examples:
- new dialogue
- altered Burrow Post outcomes
- memory-aware branches
- shadow versions
- recovered records
- faction reinterpretations

## Roster Growth Targets

These targets are not hard caps. They are planning bands.

## Phase bands

### Launch Window (0–3 months)
Target recurring content counts:
- Wanderers: **18–30**
- Runaways: **10–18**
- Wild Ones: **8–15**
- Confidants: **10–15**
- Burrowfolk class variants: **12–20**
- recurring Burrow Post templates: **40–70**

Focus:
- establish identity
- build familiarity
- teach recognition patterns
- avoid overload

### Early Live Service (3–9 months)
Target recurring content counts:
- Wanderers: **35–55**
- Runaways: **18–30**
- Wild Ones: **15–25**
- Confidants: **15–25**
- recurring Burrow Post templates: **80–140**

Focus:
- deepen chains
- introduce faction overlaps
- add Strata-specific remixes
- begin Deepening-aware content callbacks

### Mid Live Service (9–18 months)
Target recurring content counts:
- Wanderers: **55–85**
- Runaways: **30–45**
- Wild Ones: **25–40**
- Confidants: **25–35**
- recurring Burrow Post templates: **140–240**

Focus:
- progression compounding
- post-prestige reinterpretation
- stronger event crossover
- long-chain reputational callbacks

### Mature Live Service (18–30 months)
Target recurring content counts:
- Wanderers: **85–130+**
- Runaways: **45–70**
- Wild Ones: **40–60**
- Confidants: **35–50**
- recurring Burrow Post templates: **240–400+**

Focus:
- historical layering
- controlled density
- archetype branching
- memory-aware remixes
- collection completion incentives

This keeps the game in the same general *scale category* as long-running dense collector/live-ops systems without demanding that every new content source be fully handcrafted from scratch.

## Visibility Rules

### Not all recurring cast members should be equally visible
At any given time, the player’s active visible slice should be limited.

#### On-screen recommendation
At once, surface roughly:
- 3–6 active Wanderer opportunities
- 1–3 Runaway leads
- 1–2 Wild Ones problems
- 2–4 Burrow Posts of immediate relevance
- 1 highlighted chain or callback

The full library may be huge, but the active slice should stay legible.

### Priority bands
All recurring content should be sorted into visibility bands:

#### Band 1 — Immediate
Show prominently now.
Examples:
- current chain step
- event bonus target
- new clue lead
- required Deepening gate step

#### Band 2 — Suggested
Available, relevant, but not urgent.
Examples:
- optional trust step
- repeatable trade contact
- lower-priority Burrow Post

#### Band 3 — Background
Still active in simulation, but mostly not foregrounded.
Examples:
- old recurring trader
- low-tier callback encounter
- stale but repeatable source

#### Band 4 — Archived Active
Not currently visible unless filtered for, but still alive in systems.
Examples:
- completed chains with periodic refresh hooks
- earlier Strata sources with low urgency

This is how you make 100+ recurring content sources feel rich instead of chaotic.

## Wanderer Scaling Rules

Wanderers are the broadest expansion-friendly family.
They are the safest place to add volume.

### Wanderer design rules
Every Wanderer should have:
- 1 primary subtype
- 1 strain affinity
- 1 or 2 Strata anchors
- 1 reward lane
- 1 repeat rule
- 1 escalation potential

### Allowed Wanderer growth methods
Add new Wanderers through:
- new professions
- regional variants
- strain variants
- event variants
- memory-aware callback variants
- trust-chain unlock variants

### Wanderer rarity bands
- **Common recurring**: frequent traders, drifters, messengers
- **Uncommon recurring**: more conditional, clue-bearing, multi-system useful
- **Rare recurring**: weird Oddfolk, memory leaks, cross-Strata specialists
- **Legendary recurring**: world-significant, low frequency, high identity, callback-heavy

### Wanderer anti-bloat rule
Do not add a new Wanderer if it is only:
- a reskinned reward vending point
- weaker than an existing Wanderer in every way
- storyless and systemless

A new Wanderer must do at least one of:
- open a new Burrow Post pattern
- unlock a new trade behavior
- introduce a strain/faction tension
- create a new resource conversion
- deepen a known chain

---

## Runaway Scaling Rules

Runaways should scale slower than Wanderers.
They should feel more consequential.

### Runaway design rules
Every Runaway should carry at least one of:
- threat
- intrigue
- pity
- scandal
- political fallout
- system disruption

### Runaway content growth methods
Add Runaways through:
- political defections
- criminal escape lines
- noble scandals
- rogue research incidents
- prior Confidant reversals
- post-Deepening reinterpretations

### Runaway compounding rule
By mid live service, at least **30–40%** of Runaways should link to:
- old Burrow Posts
- prior Wanderers
- Clique Queue conflicts
- past Strata discoveries
- Buried Clues

Runaways should increasingly feel like consequences, not random spawns.

---

## Wild Ones Scaling Rules

The Wild Ones are the harshest roaming family and should scale through pressure, not just count.

### Wild Ones design rules
Every Wild Ones unit or crew should be defined by:
- behavior pattern
- threat lane
- pressure surface
- reward surface
- escalation tier

### Wild Ones growth methods
Add new Wild Ones through:
- role variants
- squad compositions
- pressure effects
- region remixes
- seasonal variants
- history-aware elite variants

### Threat curve rule
Do not make all later Wild Ones simply larger-stat versions.
Growth should rotate between:
- smarter disruption
- rarer timing windows
- economic pressure
- clue interference
- travel denial
- post-loss consequences

This preserves design space long term.

---

## Burrow Post Scaling Rules

Burrow Posts are the glue layer.
They are where a lot of long-run scale becomes manageable.

### Burrow Post expansion methods
Add Burrow Posts through:
- new templates
- new senders
- new condition checks
- new reward outcomes
- new chain states
- old-post remixes after Deepening

### Template ratio rule
At all times, maintain a roughly healthy mix:
- **35% repeatable utility posts**
- **25% chain progression posts**
- **15% clue or reveal posts**
- **15% event-linked posts**
- **10% rare/weird/Smoke Whispers-linked posts**

### Sender diversity rule
No single content family should dominate Post identity for too long.
Healthy sender distribution over time:
- Wanderers
- Runaways
- Wild Ones
- Confidants
- Clique members
- system notices
- unknown or memory-source senders

### Callback rule
After the first 40–60 Post templates, new Burrow Posts should increasingly do one of:
- call back to a prior post
- escalate a familiar sender
- reinterpret a resolved situation
- give partial contradiction to older history
- create uncertainty about the Deepening timeline

## Event Scaling Rules

Weekly events should scale by **rotation depth**, not endless reinvention.

### Event lane growth rule
For each of the 3 main event weeks:
- add new featured cast hooks
- add new reward bands
- add new board/focus variations
- add new light side-shop rotations
- add new callback modifiers
- keep the core rules readable

### Festival Ledger rule
Festival Ledger should scale through:
- mark thresholds
- milestone tracks
- seasonal themes
- bonus ties to older content

It should not require a new rules explanation every season.

### Event cast integration rule
By mature live service, each major event cycle should include:
- 6–10 active familiar cast callbacks
- 2–4 new cast hooks
- 1 rare story oddity
- 1 future-thread teaser or memory contradiction

This keeps the world feeling cumulative.

## Difficulty Compounding Rules

The user’s note about long-run visitor accumulation is important:
volume alone is not enough. The cast must become more layered and harder to fully optimize.

### Compounding difficulty should come from:
- more conflicting opportunities
- denser scheduling tradeoffs
- chain overlap
- stronger clue dependency webs
- more risk in picking the wrong contact first
- more consequence-bearing Runaways and Wild Ones
- more conditional access to premium chains

### Difficulty should **not** come mainly from:
- bloated text
- random stat inflation everywhere
- permanent busywork
- overwhelming alert spam
- forced completion of every cast member

### Late-game optimization rule
A veteran player should feel:
- “I know these systems better now”
- while also feeling
- “I cannot trivially maximize all of them at once”

That is healthy compounding difficulty.

---

## Deepening-Aware Content Rules

Deepening should be the main long-run multiplier for content meaning.

### Every major recurring family should eventually support:
- pre-Deepening version
- post-Deepening reinterpretation
- memory-aware contradiction
- recovered-history branch
- repeat with changed motive

### Recommended Deepening layering thresholds
By:
- **First Deepening**: light callback content begins
- **Second Deepening**: recovered records alter old assumptions
- **Third Deepening**: old contacts contradict old truths
- **Fourth Deepening**: memory instability and faction split truths emerge
- **Fifth Deepening**: older content becomes historically multi-readable

This is how the same cast can feel bigger over time without requiring an impossible volume of brand-new writing.

## Content Brick Structure

Every scalable content source should be built as a content brick with standard fields.

## Minimum recommended fields
- internal ID
- display name
- family (Wanderer / Runaway / Wild Ones / etc.)
- subtype
- strain affinity
- strata affinity
- unlock band
- trigger conditions
- recurrence rule
- post sender behavior
- reward lane
- escalation level
- callback hooks
- Deepening flags
- event flags
- trust relevance
- clue relevance
- retirement/archival behavior

A strict brick format is what makes 100+ recurring roaming content sources manageable.

---

## Unlock Band Rules

All recurring content should be assigned to one of these bands.

### Band A — onboarding familiarity
Introduces readable patterns and useful rewards.

### Band B — early differentiation
Adds personality, low drama, and basic choice tension.

### Band C — layered overlap
Begins multi-system interactions and small contradictions.

### Band D — consequence density
Adds cost, risk, and longer chain resolution.

### Band E — historical distortion
Adds post-Deepening reinterpretation, political weight, and memory weirdness.

New content should be placed in unlock bands intentionally.
Do not dump everything into visible availability just because it exists.

---

## Repetition and Variant Rules

### Every recurring content source should have at least one variant strategy
Allowed variant types:
- reward variant
- tone variant
- location variant
- timing variant
- sender variant
- strain-affinity variant
- Deepening variant
- event variant

### Recommended variant ladder
- launch with base version
- add minor reward/tone remixes
- later add chain extensions
- later add memory-aware reinterpretations
- later add prestige-aware contradictions

This is much more sustainable than shipping only one-off handcrafted entries.

---

## Naming and Catalog Rules

### Naming rule
Recurring content names must be:
- distinct at a glance
- easy to sort internally
- easy to theme by family
- reusable for variants

### Catalog rule
Maintain internal tags for:
- family
- subtype
- region
- strain
- reward lane
- progression band
- visibility band
- Deepening relevance

Without consistent cataloging, scale becomes chaos.

---

## Retirement and Archive Rules

Not every old content source needs to stay foregrounded forever.

### Content may move to archive-active state when:
- chain is completed
- rewards are mostly exhausted
- stronger successors exist
- it is historically obsolete
- the player has outgrown its urgency

### Archive-active content should still be able to:
- return in event cycles
- reappear via callback
- be referenced in new posts
- contribute to completion systems
- gain new Deepening meaning

Archive is not deletion. Archive is controlled dormancy.

---

## Quality Control Rules

Before adding any new recurring content source, ask:

1. What existing system does it enrich?
2. What content family does it belong to?
3. What player choice does it create?
4. What reward lane justifies it?
5. Does it have a recurrence plan?
6. Does it have a later callback path?
7. Does it crowd out something stronger?
8. Can it survive after the next Deepening?

If those answers are weak, the content is probably not ready.

---

## Metrics and Tracking Recommendations

To keep the live-service cast scalable, track at least:
- appearance rate
- completion rate
- abandonment rate
- repeat interaction rate
- chain progression rate
- trust progression rate
- reward claim efficiency
- archive transition rate
- event crossover participation
- Deepening callback engagement

This lets the team know whether the content library is becoming richer or just louder.

---

## Red Flags

The design is drifting if any of these become common:
- too many equally urgent posts
- new cast members that feel interchangeable
- old content never referenced again
- late-game content only scaling through numbers
- player confusion over who matters now
- event cycles feeling detached from the roaming cast
- Deepening not changing old interpretations
- internal content tracking becoming inconsistent

---

## Final Rule

The content library should grow like a **buried city**:
not by replacing older layers, but by building new passages, collapsed routes, recovered chambers, and revised histories on top of them.

The player should feel that the world is getting:
- more familiar
- more suspicious
- more layered
- more politically tangled
- more historically unstable

That is the target feeling of good long-run content scaling in **Gnome Survivor**.
