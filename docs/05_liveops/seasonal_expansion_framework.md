# Seasonal Expansion Framework

## Purpose

This document converts the project's long-run scaling rules into a practical **12–24 month live-service release framework** for **Gnome Survivor**.

It explains how the game should expand over time without:
- overwhelming the player with too many new systems at once
- invalidating older Strata, Wanderers, Runaways, or Burrow Posts
- making events feel disconnected from Deepening and story progression
- forcing every season to reinvent the game from scratch

## Core Philosophy

### Rule 1 — Seasons should reveal, not just add
A good season should not feel like a random pile of new content.
Each season should reveal:
- a new social angle
- a new historical contradiction
- a new roaming-content layer
- a new pressure on old systems
- a new interpretation of one Stratum, Strain, or Deepening beat

### Rule 2 — Every season must have 1 anchor, 2 echoes, 3 reusable bricks
Each season should contain:
- **1 anchor**: the headline theme players remember
- **2 echoes**: supporting additions that reinforce the anchor
- **3 reusable bricks**: modular templates that can be remixed later

Example:
- Anchor: Hushline memory leak season
- Echo 1: new Smoke Whispers chain
- Echo 2: Memory Fen Burrow Post escalation
- Reusable bricks: 4 new Wanderers, 3 new Burrow Posts, 1 Treasure Week sub-track

### Rule 3 — Expand horizontally before vertically, then vertically before permanently
For the first year, seasons should mostly add:
- cast breadth
- event variations
- post libraries
- region reinterpretations

After that, seasons should increasingly add:
- trust chains
- Deepening-aware remixes
- memory-state branching
- old-content reinterpretations
- cross-Strata contradictions

### Rule 4 — A season must touch at least 3 existing systems
No season should be siloed.
A season should affect at least 3 of:
- Burrow Posts
- Wanderers / Runaways / The Wild Ones
- events
- Treasures
- Clothes
- Strains
- Confidants
- Clique Queue
- The Crack
- Deepening

### Rule 5 — Seasonal content must create future hooks
Every season should leave behind at least one of these:
- a recurring NPC
- a recurring Post template
- a new event modifier
- a new Deepening contradiction
- a reusable reward lane
- a future region reinterpretation hook

## Release Cadence Model

## Recommended cadence
Use a repeating live-service cycle built around **major seasons** and **minor beats**.

### Major season cadence
- one major season every **8–10 weeks**
- each major season lasts **6 weeks of active emphasis**
- final **2–4 weeks** act as overlap, reruns, cleanup, and forward hook placement

### Minor beat cadence
Within each major season:
- 1 featured **Lucky Draw Week**
- 1 featured **Treasure Week**
- 1 featured **Echo Week**
- 1 ongoing **Festival Ledger** track
- 1 roaming cast focus rotation
- 1 Burrow Post family focus rotation

This keeps the weekly loop stable while the seasonal layer changes meaning.

## Seasonal Structure Template

Each season should contain the following sections.

### 1. Season identity
- season name
- headline strain / strata / contradiction
- tone statement
- one-line public pitch
- one-line buried truth

### 2. Cast additions
Recommended per season:
- Wanderers: **4–8**
- Runaways: **2–5**
- Wild Ones cells or named agents: **2–4**
- Confidants: **1–2 major additions** or **2–4 trust-stage expansions**

### 3. Burrow Post additions
Recommended per season:
- Burrow Posts: **10–20 new templates or variants**
- Stranger Posts: **6–12**
- Confidant Posts: **4–8**
- Wild Posts: **3–6**
- Buried Clues: **2–5**
- Smoke Whispers chains: **2–4**

### 4. Event modifications
Recommended per season:
- Lucky Draw Week: **1 new reward banner lane**
- Treasure Week: **1 new target family or exchange modifier**
- Echo Week: **1 new Echo Watch scoring modifier**
- Festival Ledger: **1 new season track wrapper**

### 5. Progression layer additions
Recommended per season:
- 1 new Treasure family subset or subtheme
- 1 new Clothes subset or appearance motif
- 1 new Strain interaction modifier
- 1 new Clique Queue pressure type
- optional Crack expansion node every 2–3 seasons

### 6. Legacy hooks
Each season should leave behind:
- at least 2 recurring content sources
- at least 1 future Deepening callback
- at least 1 future Burrow Post escalation hook

## Year 1 Seasonal Framework

Year 1 should establish identity, compounding familiarity, and system readability.
It should not try to add every possible mode or region layer.

## Season 1 — The First Stirring
**Anchor:** the world is moving again

Purpose:
- stabilize launch understanding
- introduce the first real sense of recurring cast density
- teach that Wanderers and Posts are not one-off filler

Focus systems:
- Loamwake
- Freeroot
- Burrow Posts
- Lucky Draw Week
- Daily Duties

Recommended additions:
- Wanderers: 4–6
- Runaways: 2–3
- Wild Ones: 2
- Posts: 10–15
- Buried Clues: 1–2

Narrative angle:
- early reports of movement between Burrows
- food pressure, rumor pressure, and soft political pressure
- first signs that Strata history is not being told honestly

Leave-behind hooks:
- 2 recurring Loamwake Wanderers
- 1 first recurring Wild Ones crew
- 1 Buried Clue chain leading toward Ledgerhollow

---

## Season 2 — The Record Bends
**Anchor:** archives do not agree

Purpose:
- deepen Inkroot identity
- show that records, history, and evidence are contested resources
- increase Treasure Week depth

Focus systems:
- Ledgerhollow
- Inkroot
- Treasure Week
- Treasure Finder
- Lore Ledger

Recommended additions:
- Wanderers: 5–7
- Runaways: 3–4
- Wild Ones: 2–3
- Posts: 12–18
- Buried Clues: 2–3
- Smoke Whispers: 1–2

Narrative angle:
- contradictory records surface
- archivists disagree on what started the collapse
- rogue researchers and Loose Quills become more visible

Leave-behind hooks:
- 1 recurring archive dispute system
- 1 Inkroot trust chain
- 1 Hidden Court reference for later Deepening use

---

## Season 3 — Brass in the Walls
**Anchor:** the machine ruins are still alive

Purpose:
- foreground Brassroot pragmatism, labor, and propaganda
- make infrastructure and Wayworks feel important
- create stronger Burrow production ties

Focus systems:
- Wayworks
- Brassroot
- Burrow Rushes
- Cloth Assembly
- Salvage Shed
- Crack Market

Recommended additions:
- Wanderers: 4–6
- Runaways: 3–5
- Wild Ones: 3–4
- Posts: 12–20
- Crack content variants: 2–4

Narrative angle:
- old industrial systems reactivate
- rival crews compete over salvage and transit control
- mainstream narratives about “order” begin to fracture

Leave-behind hooks:
- 1 recurring transit/logistics event modifier
- 2 salvage-themed Wild Posts
- 1 new Crack economy post chain

---

## Season 4 — Murmurs in Memory Fen
**Anchor:** memory is leaking before it should

Purpose:
- introduce Hushline unease earlier without fully explaining it
- strengthen Echo Week identity
- make Smoke Whispers feel scalable

Focus systems:
- Memory Fen
- Hushline
- Echo Week
- Echo Watch
- Gnome Pipe
- Smoke Whispers

Recommended additions:
- Wanderers: 5–7
- Runaways: 2–4
- Wild Ones: 2–3
- Posts: 10–16
- Smoke Whispers: 3–5 chains

Narrative angle:
- visions repeat details the player should not know yet
- ritual spaces begin echoing future Deepening states
- some Wanderers seem to recognize the Gnome Survivor twice

Leave-behind hooks:
- 1 persistent memory-anomaly flag
- 1 recurring Witness Confidant thread
- 1 Memory Shift boss foreshadowing chain

---

## Season 5 — The Recall Begins
**Anchor:** the old political order had backups

Purpose:
- escalate Crownroot political intrigue
- stress Clique fiction and Burrow power circles
- connect Confidant roles to historical cover-ups

Focus systems:
- Crownroot
- Clique Queue / Clique Call
- Burrow Monitor
- Standing Shelf
- High Confidants / Brokers / Witnesses

Recommended additions:
- Wanderers: 4–6
- Runaways: 4–6
- Wild Ones: 3–4
- Posts: 14–20
- Confidant expansions: 2–4

Narrative angle:
- political families appear to have preserved old fallback protocols
- Burrow offices, records, and succession claims begin reactivating
- some Court Stewards would have hidden the truth; some High Confidants want it exposed

Leave-behind hooks:
- 1 recurring court-petition content family
- 1 new Broker trust chain
- 1 future Buried Court gate clue

---

## Season 6 — Before the Court Opens
**Anchor:** everything points downward

Purpose:
- prepare the transition into later-year Deepening escalation
- unify Strata threads into one convergence pattern
- make Buried Clues feel like a cross-system language

Focus systems:
- Buried Clues
- Deepening Gate
- Buried Requirement
- The Buried Court
- cross-Strata callbacks

Recommended additions:
- Wanderers: 3–5
- Runaways: 3–4
- Wild Ones: 2–3
- Posts: 10–16
- Buried Clues: 3–5 major chains

Narrative angle:
- the player can now see that the factions were never truly separate
- old truths, false records, and machine echoes collapse into the same buried line
- the first year closes by proving the world is not recovering; it is remembering

Leave-behind hooks:
- major Deepening-era contradiction table
- recurring memory-aware Wanderers
- first stable route into post-Year-1 reinterpretation content

## Year 2 Seasonal Framework

Year 2 should shift from “the world is strange” to “the world remembers and responds.”
The goal is not raw volume alone.
The goal is **historical layering**.

### Year 2 priorities
- deepen recurring cast instead of only adding new names
- add post-Deepening variants to older content
- increase Confidant trust-path branching
- make Wanderers and Runaways contradict themselves across memory states
- expand The Crack and The Ascender as seasonal side-pressure lanes
- begin introducing factions or cast members who are aware of previous states

## Recommended Year 2 season bands
Rather than naming every season immediately, use these bands.

### Band A — Reinterpretation seasons
Purpose:
- revisit existing Strata with altered truth states
- add memory-aware remixes
- create new value from old content libraries

### Band B — Contradiction seasons
Purpose:
- pit two Strains or two historical narratives against each other
- add political pressure and Burrow Monitor stakes
- expand Clique Queue consequences

### Band C — Excavation seasons
Purpose:
- focus on The Crack, salvage lines, and buried machine-era truths
- add Crack Coins sinks and deeper resource chokes
- tie old tech back to the original collapse

### Band D — Ascender seasons
Purpose:
- escalate The Ascender repair arc
- add skyward myths, records, and power claims
- hint at future domain content without replacing current loops

### Band E — Memory Shift seasons
Purpose:
- make Shift Wardens, Remembered Wardens, and Hushline contradictions more central
- add alternate version Posts and Confidant reactions
- begin blending present-state and remembered-state content

## Seasonal Content Ratios

To keep the game readable, new content should follow rough ratios.

### Launch-to-Year-1 ratio
Per season:
- **40%** reusable cast/content expansion
- **25%** event-layer changes
- **15%** economy/progression changes
- **10%** narrative escalation
- **10%** permanent legacy hooks

### Year-2 ratio
Per season:
- **25%** new cast/content expansion
- **20%** event-layer changes
- **20%** reinterpretation of older content
- **20%** narrative escalation
- **15%** permanent legacy hooks

This shifts the game from simple additive growth into compounding reinterpretation.

## Seasonal Reward Design Rules

### Rule 1 — Seasonal rewards should widen play, not hard-lock it
Season rewards should encourage:
- trying new Strata loops
- revisiting Burrow Posts
- interacting with Confidants
- completing event side tracks

They should not hard-force one meta path.

### Rule 2 — Every season should reward 3 lanes
Each season should reward:
- one progression lane
- one collection lane
- one identity/vanity lane

Examples:
- progression: Strain Seeds, Treasure Shards, Strata Seals
- collection: Treasures, Clothes variants, lore fragments
- identity/vanity: Clique trims, Pathwheel skins, Burrow cosmetics

### Rule 3 — Seasonal currencies should not explode in number
Prefer modifiers on existing currencies over brand-new short-lived currency types.

Good seasonal additions:
- bonus Festival Marks sources
- seasonal Echo Shard exchange
- special Treasure Ticket routing
- temporary Crack Market stock

Bad seasonal additions:
- four totally isolated season currencies that never return

## Seasonal Expansion Safety Rules

### Rule 1 — No more than one major new permanent system per 2–3 seasons
Examples of major permanent systems:
- major Crack expansion tier
- new Confidant progression layer
- new Ascender subsystem
- Memory Shift progression branch

Everything else should largely be layered onto existing systems.

### Rule 2 — New cast must be introduced through existing reading surfaces
Additions should appear through:
- Burrow Posts
- Wanderer encounters
- event side tracks
- Duties
- Clique Queue
- Buried Clues

Do not introduce major cast blocks only through a giant one-time lore dump.

### Rule 3 — A season should not require mastery of every prior season
Old hooks should matter, but each season must remain playable as a readable slice.

### Rule 4 — Every 3 seasons, run a consolidation season
A consolidation season should:
- revive older cast members
- reroute stale event templates
- reward missed old chains
- summarize contradictions
- reduce player fear of permanent exclusion

## Content Pipeline Recommendations

For production, each season should be built from reusable packets.

### Core packet types
- **Cast Packet** — Wanderers, Runaways, Wild Ones, Burrowfolk, Confidants
- **Post Packet** — Burrow Posts, Stranger Posts, Confidant Posts, Wild Posts
- **Event Packet** — Lucky Draw Week, Treasure Week, Echo Week variants
- **Reward Packet** — Treasures, Clothes, Shards, Marks, Seals, cosmetic variants
- **Narrative Packet** — Buried Clues, Smoke Whispers, Deepening references
- **Legacy Packet** — future callbacks, recurring hooks, reinterpretation flags

Each season should assemble a set of packets rather than being authored as one giant monolith.

## Metrics to Watch Per Season

Track at least these:
- number of recurring cast members actually re-seen by active players
- Burrow Post open rate
- completion rate of seasonal Duties
- event participation by week type
- trust progression on new and old Confidants
- percentage of seasonal cast that recur after season end
- percentage of players who engage with at least one legacy hook
- confusion rate around new terms, systems, or currencies

If a season performs poorly, do not only add more content.
Check whether:
- the anchor was unclear
- the cast slice was too broad
- the Burrow Posts were too noisy
- event modifiers were too disconnected
- the legacy hooks were too invisible

## Recommended Immediate Use

Use this framework to plan the first **6 seasonal releases** after launch.
For each one, create a one-page seasonal brief with:
- season name
- anchor statement
- public pitch
- buried truth
- cast additions
- post additions
- event modifications
- reward lanes
- deepening hooks
- legacy hooks

That will give the project a scalable live-service roadmap without losing the tone and system cohesion already established in the design bible.
