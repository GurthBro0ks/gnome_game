# Season Briefs Pack

## Purpose

This file turns the seasonal framework into the first **6 concrete season briefs** for **Gnome Survivor**.

These are not final scripts or balance sheets. They are build-facing one-pagers meant to align:
- narrative anchor
- featured Strata and Strains

- roaming cast additions
- Burrow Post families

# How to use these briefs

Each brief is designed to answer 8 questions quickly:
1. What is the season about?
2. What truth or contradiction does it reveal?
3. Which systems are most affected?
4. Which roaming-content families expand?
5. What Burrow Post families should be added?
6. How do weekly events get reskinned or modified?
7. What rewards reinforce the theme?
8. What persistent hooks should remain after the season ends?

# Season 1 — First Stirring

## Public pitch
The paths between Burrows are busy again. Peddlers travel farther, Drifters arrive hungrier, and the first new Clique tensions begin to surface.

## Buried truth
Movement is not returning naturally. Something is driving small populations to relocate, disappear, and reclassify old routes.

## Featured focus
- Stratum: **Loamwake**
- Strain emphasis: **Freeroot**
- Deepening tie: **First Deepening: The Stirring**
- Event emphasis: **Lucky Draw Week**

## Tone
Warm, busy, slightly suspicious. A season about “normal life resuming” while the player starts noticing that the normal life is crooked.

## System focus
- Burrow Posts
- Wanderers
- Daily Duties
- Travel Pack / Field Returns
- Lucky Wheel
- Clique Queue

## New cast targets
- Wanderers: **6**
- Runaways: **2**
- Wild Ones cells: **1** named crew + 1 recurring rumor chain
- Confidants: **1 Freeroot Pathfinder**

## Suggested Wanderer spread
- 2 Peddlers
- 1 Pilgrim
- 1 Drifter
- 1 Messenger
- 1 Oddfolk

## Burrow Post families to add
- food shortage posts
- travel permit disputes
- small-route rumor chains
- first-contact neighbor Burrow requests
- missing supply-cart notices
- low-stakes Wild Posts that escalate later

## Event modifications
### Lucky Draw Week
Theme: roadside luck, scavenged charm, petty barter prizes
- banner rewards lean toward starter Clothes, early Treasures, Mushcaps, and route tools
- Lucky Stall includes one recurring route-motif cosmetic track

### Treasure Week
Theme: roadside finds and “common history” objects
- Treasure Exchange focuses on humble objects with suspicious provenance

### Echo Week
Theme: overheard road memories
- Echo Watch focuses on simple memory fragments about travel and resettlement

### Festival Ledger wrapper
Seasonal track theme: **Open Roads, Closed Mouths**

## Reward motifs
- patched travel wear
- road-sign Treasures
- supply ledgers
- preserved snacks / brew kits
- early Pathwheel cosmetics

## Leave-behind hooks
- 2 recurring Loamwake Wanderers
- 1 first “everyone knows them” Peddler pair
- 1 Wild Ones rumor tag that keeps appearing in later Wild Posts
- 1 Buried Clue chain pointing toward Ledgerhollow records inconsistency

# Season 6 — The Recall War

## Public pitch
Old records, remembered routes, and cross-Strata witnesses finally start agreeing on one thing: the collapse was not accidental.

## Buried truth
The old ruling blocs accelerated machine catastrophe on purpose once they believed they could survive it better than everyone else.

## Featured focus
- Cross-Strata season: **Loamwake / Ledgerhollow / Memory Fen / Wayworks / The Buried Court**
- Strain emphasis: all five, with **Hushline** and **Crownroot** conflict foregrounded
- Deepening tie: **Fifth Deepening: The Buried Court**
- Event emphasis: all three weeks receive season-wide contradiction modifiers

## Tone
Revelatory, unstable, triumphant in short bursts, then grimly funny. The season should feel like the player is finally seeing the machine underneath the fairy tale.

## System focus
- Deepening Gate
- Buried Requirement chains
- Burrow Monitor
- Buried Clues mega-arc
- Clique Queue pressure escalations
- multi-family Burrow Post convergence

## New cast targets
- Wanderers: **10**
- Runaways: **6**
- Wild Ones cells: **4**
- Confidants: **2 major trust expansions**, no mandatory brand-new role required

## Suggested cast structure
- 2 returning Wanderers from Seasons 1–3 with changed context
- 2 returning Runaways whose earlier labels were incomplete or false
- 2 Crownroot-facing insiders
- 2 Hushline-adjacent impossible witnesses
- 2 cross-Strata logistics figures who prove the route network was manipulated

## Burrow Post families to add
- records that unlock only when paired with earlier season posts
- contradictory emergency decrees
- recovered machine-catastrophe planning logs
- false amnesty offers
- hidden Wild Ones handler notices
- Buried Requirement stage posts that feel administrative until they suddenly are not

## Event modifications
### Lucky Draw Week
Theme: destabilized public morale
- banner includes callback rewards from earlier seasons with “corrected” lore tags

### Treasure Week
Theme: provenance collapse
- Treasure Exchange can temporarily surface competing truth-lanes from older treasures

### Echo Week
Theme: memory convergence
- Echo Watch uses cross-season combo scoring and callbacks to prior Echo chains

### Festival Ledger wrapper
Seasonal track theme: **Everyone Remembered Too Late**

## Reward motifs
- fractured heraldry
- machine ruin décor
- cross-Strata patchwork clothing
- recovered command insignia
- end-of-year callback cosmetics built from older season motifs

## Leave-behind hooks
- permanent long-tail mega-chain for future years
- a stable cast of recognizable recurring Wanderers / Runaways / Wild Ones that can support 100+ growth
- one enduring truth: every new season must reinterpret the old world, not merely add another layer of collectible noise

# Recommended production order

If you are building these briefs into actual content, use this order:
1. Season 1 — First Stirring
2. Season 2 — The Record Bends
3. Season 4 — Brass in Motion
4. Season 3 — Fen of Echoes
5. Season 5 — Court of Splendor, Court of Rot
6. Season 6 — The Recall War

Reason:
- Seasons 1 and 2 establish cast, routes, and records
- Season 4 broadens physical/world systems cleanly
- Season 3 deepens the mystery once the player already trusts the surface systems
- Season 5 raises elite political stakes
- Season 6 acts as the first major convergence season

# Expansion note

This pack is intentionally written for a game that can support **years of cast growth**, not a tiny one-off campaign.

These six seasons should establish:
- recurring Wanderer logic
- reusable Runaway escalation lanes
- The Wild Ones as scalable antagonistic pressure
- Post libraries that can multiply without losing identity
- event wrappers that change meaning without changing the weekly skeleton
- a content structure that can grow toward **100+ recurring roaming content sources** over time
