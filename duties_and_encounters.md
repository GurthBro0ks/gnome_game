# Gnome Game — Duties and Encounters

## Purpose
This document defines the reusable authored-content layer for the project:
- **Duties** as the main quest/content umbrella
- **Encounters** as the moment-to-moment authored packets that make the Strata feel alive
- content categories for **Burrow Posts**, **Confidant Posts**, **Stranger Posts**, **Wild Posts**, **Smoke Whispers**, and **Buried Clues**
- reusable templates that can be produced at scale without flattening tone

This file is meant to bridge worldbuilding and production.
It should help writers, system designers, UI designers, and economy designers all talk about the same content bricks.

It assumes the current canon from:
- `master_glossary.md`
- `phase1_content_bible.md`
- `strains_bible.md`
- `clique_confidants_bible.md`
- `deepening_arc.md`

---

## Core rule
A **Duty** is a readable player objective.
An **Encounter** is the authored situation wrapped around it.

That means:
- Duties are the trackable layer
- Encounters are the dramatic layer
- Posts are the delivery layer
- Rewards are the reinforcement layer

The player should feel like they are not merely clearing chores.
They are navigating a half-broken civilization that presents all of its crises as paperwork, favors, rumors, rituals, and strange little emergencies.

---

## Design goals
All Duties and Encounters should support at least one of these goals:

1. **Make the world feel socially alive**
2. **Expose strain politics through play, not exposition**
3. **Teach a system without feeling like a tutorial box**
4. **Provide reusable content bricks for live expansion**
5. **Reward attention, not just repetition**
6. **Let humor and dread coexist**

---

## Master content ladder

### Tier 1 — Surface duties
Simple tasks with light fiction wrappers.
Used heavily in onboarding and daily loops.

Examples:
- gather from Dewpond
- answer a Burrow Post
- deliver goods to a Wanderer
- inspect a clue in Loamwake

### Tier 2 — Social duties
Tasks tied to Cliques, Confidants, Burrowfolk, and Strata tensions.
These should feel more specific and political.

Examples:
- settle a Clique Queue dispute
- escort a Messenger through Wayworks
- retrieve a sealed note for an Archivist
- patch up Burrowfolk after a Warden Clash

### Tier 3 — Strange duties
Tasks that expose instability, contradiction, or hidden truth.
These should begin to hint at the Buried Requirement and later Deepening layers.

Examples:
- follow a Smoke Whisper
- verify a Buried Clue no one wants verified
- track a Dissident who remembers the wrong version of events
- test a Strain Loom anomaly

### Tier 4 — Deepening duties
Tasks that alter player understanding of the world.
These should feel rare, consequential, and layered.

Examples:
- reconstruct a falsified ledger
- confront a Remembered Warden
- decide whether to publish a forbidden record
- choose which Clique receives a truth that could destabilize a Stratum

## Encounter categories

### Burrow Encounters
Everyday problems presented as normal civic life.
These should be funny, local, and slightly sad.

Examples:
- a Clique is politely fighting over resource distribution
- Dewpond water tastes wrong but nobody wants to file it properly
- a Steward posted the same request three different ways to avoid blame

### Strata Encounters
Region-specific authored packets.
These should reinforce each Stratum’s identity.

Examples:
- Loamwake: wandering brewer caravan with spoiled cap stock
- Ledgerhollow: sealed archive shelf with mismatched records
- Memory Fen: ritual path that keeps changing after dusk
- Wayworks: route dispute between practical engineers and official routekeepers
- The Buried Court: elegant proceedings over facts that clearly should not exist

### Confidant Encounters
Character-focused packets tied to Trust, Calling, and Confidence Trail.

Examples:
- a Fixer asks for parts with suspicious urgency
- an Archivist needs verification before releasing a record
- a High Confidant asks for discretion, not help

### Wanderer Encounters
Short social packets with trade, rumor, side favors, or quiet warnings.

Examples:
- a Peddler offers a bargain that only makes sense if they know your route
- a Pilgrim wants help reaching a memorial that may not be real
- an Oddfolk tells a joke that doubles as a warning about Memory Shift

### Runaway Encounters
Fleeing, hiding, bargaining, or unraveling packets.

Examples:
- a Defector offers records in exchange for shelter
- a Breakaway insists the posted charges are fabricated
- a Loose Quill wants access to a Strain Loom without oversight

### Wild Encounters
Urgent, dangerous, or escalating packets tied to The Wild Ones.

Examples:
- a Fang squad arrives before the paperwork does
- Trackers are following the wrong target on purpose
- Rousers turn a quiet resource complaint into an armed mess

### Smoke Whispers
Hidden, eerie, humorous, or destabilizing micro-events triggered through the Gnome Pipe.

Examples:
- a calm little voice gives a fact no living clerk should know
- a dead route marker appears in the wrong Stratum
- an ancestor figure gives practical advice that sounds like sabotage

### Buried Clues
Persistent discovery objects or event chains that connect to Deepening and Buried Requirement progress.

Examples:
- a ledger copy that contradicts every official version
- an old map keyed to a Stratum that should not yet exist
- a ceremonial object with a machine serial under the gilding

## Delivery surfaces

### Burrow Posts
Default civic and practical duties.
Tone:
- routine
- lightly bureaucratic
- local
- plausible deniability everywhere

Best content types:
- gathering duties
- repair duties
- local disputes
- ordinary requests that hide bigger problems

### Confidant Posts
Character-specific asks that build Trust and Confidence Trail progress.
Tone:
- more personal
- more pointed
- more revealing

Best content types:
- private favors
- strained loyalty asks
- moral tests
- role-specific tasks

### Stranger Posts
Wanderer-delivered opportunities, rumors, trades, and requests.
Tone:
- transient
- opportunistic
- sometimes generous, sometimes suspicious

Best content types:
- escorts
- trades
- rumors
- odd one-off encounters

### Wild Posts
Urgent notices involving The Wild Ones, Runaways, or destabilizing events.
Tone:
- sharper
- more immediate
- more dangerous

Best content types:
- pursuit duties
- threat notices
- false reports
- political cleanups

### Smoke Whispers
Not really a post surface, but a hidden triggered-content lane.
Tone:
- intimate
- surreal
- half-helpful
- half-ominous

Best content types:
- hidden micro-encounters
- ancestor humor
- contradictory memory fragments
- hidden route unlocks

## Recommended content template
Use this template for nearly all authored packets.

```text
Title:
Surface:
Category:
Primary Stratum:
Primary Strain:
Featured NPC type:
Hook:
Player Objective:
Twist:
Resolution Options:
Reward Types:
Follow-up Flag:
Tone Notes:
```

### Notes on each field
- **Title**: short, memorable, slightly in-world
- **Surface**: Burrow Posts / Confidant Posts / Stranger Posts / Wild Posts / Smoke Whispers / Buried Clues
- **Category**: Gathering / Repair / Investigation / Escort / Ritual / Dispute / Pursuit / Deepening
- **Primary Stratum**: where it belongs first
- **Primary Strain**: whose worldview is being expressed or challenged
- **Featured NPC type**: Wanderer subtype, Confidant role, Burrowfolk class, Runaway type, etc.
- **Hook**: why the player notices it
- **Player Objective**: the readable task
- **Twist**: the authored reveal
- **Resolution Options**: 1 to 3 outcomes, even if only one is active at launch
- **Reward Types**: gameplay and narrative reward layer
- **Follow-up Flag**: whether it chains later
- **Tone Notes**: funny, tense, tragic, surreal, petty, etc.

## Tone rules by surface

### Burrow Posts
- practical first
- funny through phrasing, not punchlines
- always sound like someone is trying to keep a problem small

### Confidant Posts
- more emotionally specific
- reveal character through what they omit
- should often ask for help that is not purely mechanical

### Stranger Posts
- should create movement
- should imply a bigger world outside the current route
- can be generous, but rarely for free

### Wild Posts
- should feel like escalation
- can be wrong, manipulated, or politically motivated
- should increase pressure even when the player ignores them

### Smoke Whispers
- should be short and sharp
- should not over-explain the mystery
- should make the player feel rewarded for noticing patterns

### Buried Clues
- should move story truth forward
- should always destabilize at least one public story
- should connect upward into Deepening

## Reward logic
Each Duty or Encounter should usually reward across at least two lanes:

1. **Mechanical reward**
   - Mooncaps
   - Glowcaps
   - Mushcaps
   - Field Cards
   - Treasure Shards
   - Echoes
   - Favor Marks
   - Crack Coins
   - Ascender Parts
   - Strata Seals

2. **Progress reward**
   - Trust
   - Standing
   - Confidence Trail progress
   - Buried Clue progress
   - Deepening Gate progress
   - region unlock flags

3. **Narrative reward**
   - new lore fragment
   - new NPC relationship state
   - new contradiction exposed
   - new route or follow-up flag

Launch-safe rule:
A piece of content should not require huge bespoke reward logic to feel worthwhile.
Whenever possible, narrative rewards should hang off existing currencies, progress counters, or unlock flags.

## Regional encounter guidance

### Loamwake
Use:
- easy practical duties
- Wanderer contact
- soft comedy
- hidden buffer-zone trauma

Avoid:
- making it feel shallow or purely pastoral

### Ledgerhollow
Use:
- record disputes
- archive contradictions
- survey work
- clue-heavy packets

Avoid:
- generic “library level” writing

### Memory Fen
Use:
- Echoes
- ritual absurdity
- ancestral contradiction
- Smoke Whispers

Avoid:
- pure solemn mysticism with no humor

### Wayworks
Use:
- route disputes
- escorts
- transit pressure
- practical sabotage

Avoid:
- making it a generic industrial map without social complexity

### The Buried Court
Use:
- elegant threat
- hidden hierarchy
- remembered truth
- high-status lies

Avoid:
- overloading too early with endgame mystery language

## Starter content prompts
These are reusable prompt stems for future writing.

### Burrow Post prompt stem
"Write a Burrow Post Duty set in [Stratum]. It should involve [resource/system], reveal [social tension], and end with a choice that affects [Standing/Trust/Buried Clue progress]. Keep the tone [cozy/petty/ominous]."

### Confidant Post prompt stem
"Write a Confidant Post for a [role] Confidant from [Strain]. The request should appear practical but reveal a deeper motive connected to [trust, calling, or hidden history]."

### Stranger Post prompt stem
"Write a Stranger Post involving a [Wanderer subtype] passing through [Stratum]. Include a small trade, a larger rumor, and one line that hints at a wider world beyond the current route." 

### Wild Post prompt stem
"Write a Wild Post involving [Runaway subtype] and [Wild Ones subtype]. The notice should look official but leave room for doubt about who is actually in danger." 

### Smoke Whisper prompt stem
"Write a Smoke Whisper triggered by the Gnome Pipe in [Stratum]. Keep it under 120 words. It should include one strange image, one practical hint, and one implication that history is repeating incorrectly." 

### Buried Clue prompt stem
"Write a Buried Clue event in [Stratum] that contradicts an accepted public story. Make the clue concrete, small enough to dismiss, and important enough to change how the player reads a later Deepening."
