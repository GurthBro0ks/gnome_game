=Journey to the West=
Journey to the West is the unique element to Bharata. Take a journey with Sanzang, Pigsy, Monke, and Sandy in this adventure inspired by the [//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journey_to_the_West 16th century Chinese Novel Journey to the West].

= Dharma Fight Tips =

1. Equipping LuBu as your realm trait will cause him to attack all your enemies every three rounds. It's not much, but it helps.
1. Make sure to level up all your characters, sutras, mantras, and gear.
1. It's ok to come back to secondary events later, but make sure you do them all eventually to make yourself more powerful.
1. While your snail travels through Bharata, you earn more currency for Journey to the West. It's ok to come back to Journey to the West after a few runs through Bharata (sped up or naturally run) so you can buy more gear and levels.
1. Each character has their strengths and it's best to stick to those strengths
1. Sanzang - Some damage and some buffs. Early on he is strong but as you get further he becomes less powerful and useful.
1. Monke - This is really your main character and your main damage. Once he learns 3-head Form and starts doing double attcks he'll be doing the majority of your damage.
1. Pigsy - Your healer, focus on having him heal as much as possible.
1. Sandy - Generates more rage for himself and for other characters. Try to use Sealing Punch as much as possible to heal and give rage.

1. Adversity 72
This fight can be a little tough. It's important to start developing your rotations that encourage Sandy to give Monke the most Rage. Pigsy should be healing as much as possible.

1. Adveristies 80 and 81
These final two fights are the toughest for Journey to the West. Here's a rotation to use to help tell you what you should be selecting for each character's attack.

- **Health** - affects the character's HP
- **Strength** - affects the character's Phys ATK and Phys RES
- **Spell** - affects the character's Spell ATK and Spell RES
- **Speed** - affects the character's action priority

=Gear=
You can buy and equip gear on your traveling Monks. Each Monk has 6 slots for gear: Weapon, Chest, Head, Feet, Ring, and Neck.

Most slots will contain General Gear. Each piece of General Gear can be bought for each Monk that can equip it. Monks also have two Exclusive Gear slots each that equips gear specifically made for them. Only one piece of Exclusive Gear can be bought for the Monk that can equip it.
