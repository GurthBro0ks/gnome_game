1. The Pyramid
The Pyramids are the unique element of Kemet, a feature that is similar to a tower defense minigame where waves of monsters are fought with your pyramid as you upgrade it and unlock new modules. 

The Modern Pyramid is unlocked once the first Kemet Apostle is dominated. The Past Pyramid is acquired with The Black Book INTEL and the Future Pyramid is acquired with Book of the Sun INTEL. Each Pyramid has various abilities: 

 The Modern Pyramid enhances the Snail's HARD Stats
 The Past Pyramid lowers the ATK and DEF of the enemy
 The Future Pyramid attacks the enemy on the first turn

Each has its advantages, though only one can be applied during battle. Therefore, change your leading Pyramid strategically from wave to wave. 

Each Pyramid has three parts that can be upgraded, which in turn levels up the Pyramid.

1. Upgrade Material
There are five different kinds of stones available during the exploration of Kemet, which are used to upgrade each of the three Pyramids. Each stone comes from the Quarry grids (upgraded through Kemet INTEL in either the Past, Present, Future, or victory rewards over enemies during Pyramid defense.

As the individual parts of each Pyramid are upgraded, the level of that Pyramid will increase and therefore increase its stats and abilities. Every 5 levels there is a jump in power and every 10 levels a pyramid module is gained.

1. Modern Pyramid
Initially (Lv.3), the Modern Pyramid raises the Snail's HARD Stats by +3.95% before battle. Once fully upgraded (Lv.90), the Modern Pyramid raises the Snail's HARD Stats by +30%.  

The Modern Pyramid only deals Physical and Antiair damage, so unlocking the Past Pyramid as early as possible is recommended.

1. Modules
Throughout Kemet, Modules can be collected and mounted onto the Pyramids to assist in defense. There are ATK and DEF modules, and four can be mounted at one time. However, some modules are invalid for certain waves. Whlie initially modules will seem to matter little, they can be the key factor to a successful defense later on.

1. Enemies
There are three different classes of enemy that can be encountered during defense. Each wave consists of two different enemies, typically each from a different class. During defense, the Pyramids can inflict Physical, Energy, Magic, and Antiair DMG. Enemies always have a weakness to one DMG type, and at least two invulnerabilities to other DMG types. Therefore, paying attention to enemy types and adjusting the front Pyramid and mounted modules is crucial.

Enemies can either be on the ground of flying through the air- Flying enemies will always have an Antiair weakness.
