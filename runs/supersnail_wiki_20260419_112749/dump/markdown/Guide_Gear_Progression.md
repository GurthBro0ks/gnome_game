1. General Tips for Gear
1. 1) Percent Boosts are better than flat stat boosts
An improvement of +2% Ignore DEF is better than a flat increase to ATK.  This is because in the long term, enemies will have greater and greater DEF stats.  A flat increase of +1000 ATK is not as good as lowering an enemy with DEF of 100K by 2%.  This lowering of DEF will increase the final DMG done by 2K rather than the 1K flat attack boost.  As you progress further and further in the game, the % boosts become stronger and stronger.

1. 2) Its better to have a +2 of an S-Tier gear than two +1's of any tier
This is because +1 gear only combines the effects of the two gears, and does not improve the effects.  For almost all gear, only one of the two effects is really S-Tier, so combining gear does not provide a major benefit.  Improving the effects at +2 (and beyond) is how you really improve gear.

<blockquote style="border-left: 4px solid #aaaaaa; margin-left: 0;">
-*Example:**<br>
 +0 has the effect **Ignore enemy DEF +12%**, combining it with  +0 does not improve the **Ignore enemy DEF +12%** (which is the S-Tier effect).  Once it reaches +2, the effect will increase to **Ignore enemy DEF +14%** - which is worth the extra  and s.
</blockquote>

1. 3) Reset your gear
You will have a limited number of EoH and AoW (unless you're a super-whale).  It is best to periodically dismantle your gear to switch between Farming and Progression setups.  It costs 100 W-Tads to dismantle gear into its parts.  You **DO NOT** lose any Glue, B-Tads, Orange Reagent, etc when dismantling.  You just lose the 100 W-Tads price to dismantle gear by 1 Tier.

1. Exploration Progression
If you are trying to push your level in explorations (not domination, but pushing to beat new bosses), you will want to focus on ATK and overall stats.  Many of the "First round" bonuses are not really needed because you're not focusing on domination.<br>

Below are the generally accepted Tiers and Priorities for Gear.  There are tables for Farming Gear, Form-Specific Gear, Red +0 Gear, and Red +2 (and beyond) Gear.  Generally you should avoid +1 gear because it just combines two effects on the same piece of gear, and almost all gear has one good effect and one ok/not-great effect at the +1 level.  From +2 and beyond, the good effect starts to increase.  So its better to invest your  /  /  into one +2 gear rather than two +1 gears.  You can still equip the Red gear that has the good effects at +0.<br>

Remember that you will always want to also include the **Orange/Red** gear for the domain you are exploring in addition to the gear listed below.  The Realm gear is considered S-Tier because it include a **DMG +N%** effect for the domain.<br>

1. Choosing Gear for Exploration Domination
When choosing gear for domination runs, it all comes down to damage output for the first 3 rounds.  You should sacrifice all DEF / HP in favor of ELMT DMG, ATK, and RUSH.

Stats Priority: <code>ELMT DMG > ATK > RUSH</code>

If you're trying to dominate an apostle, you will have spotted the enemy's weakness and do rush DMG as well.  Since ELMT DMG applies to both regular and rush attacks, it is the highest priority.  RUSH is lowest priority because depending on the defense of your opponent and your own RUSH stat, you may end up defaulting to the minimum (5%).  (*If you want to dive deep into combat stats, see: Combat Damage*)

1. Home Battles
For Home battles, all of the advice and guidance under Exploration Progression applies, with the exception of using any Realm gear.  Remove that from your build, change your compass relics and you're good to go.

1. Farming
-*Note:** If you're farming for an extended period of time, consider dismantling some of your progression gear to increase the rank of your Farming gear.  You don't want to miss out on the additional Travel SPD, Cells, Time Chests, etc from upgrading your farming gear.

When choosing a farming Gear setup:

1. Equip **ALL** farming gear
1.* See notes on  and  for exceptions
1. Choose a piece of Form-Specific gear that aligns with your farming needs.
1. Consider equipping Realm-specific Gear (provides INTEL +% if needed and DMG +%)
1. Equip your best gear for progression to fill the remaining slots.
